## Section 4: What to Include in Your Message

**Design:** Four-point list. White background.

**H2:** Send These Four Things — Get a Complete Quote First Time

Quotes with all four items below come back faster and more accurately. Missing any one of them means a follow-up question from the engineering team, which adds time.

**1 — Your 3D File**
STEP is preferred for CNC machining and sheet metal. STL is accepted for 3D printing. DXF works for flat profiles and laser-cut parts. If you only have a 2D drawing, a fully dimensioned PDF covers simple geometry.

**2 — Material and Grade**
Aluminium, stainless steel, titanium, ABS, PEEK — plus the specific grade where it matters (e.g. 6061 vs 7075 aluminium, 303 vs 316L stainless). Different grades have different costs, lead times, and machinability considerations. Unspecified material leads to an assumed grade on the quote.

**3 — Quantity**
Single prototype or low-volume run — state the exact number. Per-unit pricing changes at quantities of 1, 5, 10, 25, and 50. If you need pricing for multiple quantities in one quote, list them.

**4 — Tolerances and Critical Dimensions**
If your drawing has GD&T callouts or tight tolerances on specific features, highlight them. Standard CNC work follows ISO 2768-m (±0.01 mm). Tighter callouts affect process selection, setup time, and price — flagging them upfront avoids surprises at quote delivery.

## Section 6: FAQ

**Design:** Accordion. White background.

**H2:** Common Questions Before Getting in Touch

**Q1: How quickly will I receive a quote after uploading my file?**
Standard geometry returns instant pricing through the online quoting system. Complex parts — tight tolerances, unusual materials, multi-process configurations — go to engineering review and come back within 24 hours. Every quote includes DFM feedback specific to your geometry. If the team has questions about your drawing, they contact you directly before quoting rather than making assumptions.

**Q2: Can I get a quote without a 3D file?**
Yes, though the quote will be less detailed. A fully dimensioned 2D drawing in PDF covers simpler geometry. For complex parts, a 3D STEP file is strongly recommended — it removes ambiguity about geometry and lets the engineering team confirm tolerance feasibility before quoting. If you only have a sketch or concept, describe the part in the notes field and the team will advise on what's needed.

**Q3: What is the minimum order for a quote?**
No minimum. Single-part quotes are accepted for CNC machining, 3D printing, and sheet metal fabrication. Per-unit cost on single pieces is higher than batch pricing — setup time is the same regardless of quantity — but there is no order floor. State your exact quantity and the quote reflects that volume.

**Q4: Do you accept NDA before sharing design files?**
Yes. Contact the team directly to request an NDA before uploading files. All uploads through the quoting system are handled securely and treated as confidential — no files are shared with third parties. For formal NDA requirements before any file exchange, email the team first.

**Q5: Can I speak with an engineer directly about my project?**
Yes. Complex projects — multi-process assemblies, unusual materials, tight-tolerance components, or parts with regulatory requirements — benefit from a direct conversation before quoting. Use the contact form to describe your project and request an engineering discussion. The team will arrange a call at a time that works for your time zone.

**Q6: What file formats do you accept?**
STEP (.step/.stp) is preferred for all 3D parts. STL, IGES, DXF, DWG, and PDF are also accepted. SolidWorks (.sldprt), CATIA (.CATPart), and Parasolid (.x_t) work for CNC machining. For sheet metal, DXF with a bend table is ideal. If you have multiple files — 3D model plus 2D drawing — upload both. The engineering team uses both during DFM review.

**Q7: Do you ship internationally?**
Yes. TOP PROTO ships to North America, Europe, Asia, the Middle East, and beyond via DHL, FedEx, and UPS express services. Sea freight is available for larger orders. State your destination country when requesting a quote — the team includes shipping options and estimated transit times in the quote. International shipping adds 3–7 business days to the production lead time depending on destination and service level.

**Q8: How does TOP PROTO handle non-conforming parts?**
Non-conforming parts are quarantined and dispositioned through a formal Non-Conformance Report (NCR) process — not shipped to meet a deadline. Disposition options are rework, replacement, or concession — all documented. You are notified of any disposition that affects your delivery timeline before it becomes a problem. Root cause investigation runs on every NCR to prevent recurrence.
