### Group 1: Quoting and Ordering

**Q: How do I get a quote from TOP PROTO?**
Upload your STEP file on the Get a Quote page. Standard geometry returns instant pricing. Complex parts — tight tolerances, unusual materials, multi-process configurations — go to engineering review and come back within 24 hours. Every quote includes DFM feedback at no charge: wall thickness, undercuts, tolerance feasibility, and any geometry issues that affect cost or manufacturability. No separate request needed. Approve online when you're ready to proceed.

**Q: How long does a quote take?**
Standard geometry returns an instant price through the online system. Complex parts receive an engineering review within 24 hours of file upload. If the team has questions about your drawing — an ambiguous callout, a tolerance at the edge of what's practical — they contact you directly before quoting rather than making assumptions. Quote includes confirmed lead time, exact pricing, process recommendation, and DFM notes.

**Q: What file formats does TOP PROTO accept?**
STEP (.step / .stp) is the preferred format for all 3D parts — it carries full geometry and is what engineers use for toolpath planning and tolerance confirmation. STL, IGES, DXF, DWG, and PDF are also accepted. SolidWorks (.sldprt), CATIA (.CATPart), and Parasolid (.x_t) are accepted for CNC machining. For sheet metal, DXF with a bend table is ideal. When your drawing has tight-tolerance callouts or GD&T requirements, attach the 2D drawing PDF alongside the 3D STEP file — the engineering team uses both during DFM review.

**Q: What is the minimum order quantity?**
No minimum on CNC machining, 3D printing, or sheet metal fabrication. A single part is a valid order. Per-unit cost is higher on one piece than on a batch because setup time is the same regardless of quantity, but there is no order floor. Vacuum casting has a practical minimum of 5–10 units to justify silicone mould preparation. Injection molding has no formal MOQ, but tooling cost makes very small quantities uneconomical — the team flags this and suggests the appropriate process at quote stage.

**Q: Can I get quotes for multiple quantities in one request?**
Yes. State your quantities in the notes field when uploading — for example, "pricing for 1, 5, and 25 pieces." The engineering team returns per-unit pricing at each volume. Per-unit cost typically decreases at 5, 10, 25, and 50 parts as setup time is spread across more pieces.

**Q: Do you accept NDA before receiving design files?**
Yes. Contact the team before uploading. NDA is returned for signature before any file exchange. All uploads through the quoting system are handled securely and treated as confidential. Files are not shared with third parties on standard orders.

### Group 2: Tolerances and Quality

**Q: What tolerances can TOP PROTO hold on CNC machined parts?**
Standard CNC work follows ISO 2768-m — ±0.01 mm on linear dimensions, sufficient for most structural and non-critical geometry. Functional features hold to ±0.005 mm. For critical features — bearing seats, sealing surfaces, precision bores — tight-tolerance 5-axis machining achieves ±0.005 mm, confirmed by CMM and reported per feature against your drawing callout. Wire EDM reaches ±0.002–0.005 mm on 2D profiles. Every tight-tolerance order ships with a dimensional inspection report confirming actual measured values against nominal.

**Q: How does TOP PROTO verify part dimensions?**
Dimensional inspection runs at defined intervals during production — not just as a final check. Tight-tolerance orders and first articles are measured on CMM — coordinate measuring machine, which measures part geometry to micron-level accuracy. The CMM report shows measured value, nominal, tolerance, and pass/fail status per feature, referenced to your drawing revision. Every order ships with a dimensional inspection report. Material certifications traceable to heat number and a Certificate of Conformance are included as standard.

**Q: What quality certifications does TOP PROTO hold?**
TOP PROTO holds ISO 9001:2015 certification — the international standard for quality management systems, independently audited by an accredited third-party body. ISO 9001:2015 covers the full production chain: incoming material, production control, inspection, non-conformance handling, and shipping release. Certificate is available on request.

**Q: What happens if a part fails inspection?**
Non-conforming parts are quarantined and dispositioned through a formal NCR (Non-Conformance Report) process. Disposition options are rework, replacement, or concession — all documented. Non-conforming parts do not ship to meet a deadline. You are notified of any disposition that affects your delivery timeline before it becomes a problem. Root cause investigation runs on every non-conformance to prevent recurrence on subsequent orders.

**Q: Do you provide material certifications?**
Yes. Material certificates — confirming alloy grade, heat number, temper designation, and mechanical properties — are included with every order. These trace directly to the raw stock used on your job, not to a generic batch. Certificate of Conformance and dimensional inspection report ship with every order as standard.

**Q: Can you provide First Article Inspection Reports?**
Yes. FAI is available on all production orders and performed on first runs for new parts. The FAI report establishes the dimensional baseline for all subsequent production batches. Reports can be formatted to AS9102 (aerospace) or AIAG PPAP (automotive) on request. Cpk data is available for ongoing production programmes.

### Group 3: Processes and Materials

**Q: What manufacturing processes does TOP PROTO offer?**
Eight processes run in-house from one Dongguan facility: CNC machining (milling, turning, 5-axis), 3D printing (SLA, SLS, MJF, FDM), sheet metal fabrication (laser cutting, bending, welding), vacuum casting, injection molding, wire EDM, rapid prototyping, and surface finishing. One quality system covers all eight. One shipment regardless of which combination your project needs. No subcontracting on standard orders.

**Q: What materials does TOP PROTO machine and manufacture?**
CNC machining: aluminium (6061, 7075, 2024, 5052), stainless steel (303, 304, 316L, 17-4PH), titanium (Grade 2, Grade 5), brass, copper, tool steel, steel alloy (4140, 4340), bronze, and engineering plastics including PEEK, Delrin, nylon, ABS, polycarbonate, PTFE, HDPE, acrylic, and Ultem. 3D printing: SLA resins, SLS and MJF nylon (PA12), FDM thermoplastics (ABS, PETG, TPU, ASA). Sheet metal: aluminium, stainless steel, mild steel, brass, and copper. Confirm your specific grade and condition at quote stage.

**Q: What surface finishes does TOP PROTO offer?**
In-house surface finishing includes Type II and Type III hard anodizing, powder coating, passivation, nickel plating, bead blasting, black oxide, electropolishing, brushed finish, painting, and polishing. Anodizing coating thickness: 5–25 µm standard, 25–150 µm hard anodizing. Passivation per ASTM A967. Specify the finish at quote stage — the engineering team confirms compatibility with your material and geometry.

**Q: Which process is right for my prototype?**
Process selection depends on material, geometry, quantity, and required properties. CNC machining is the default for metal prototypes and precision plastic parts — tight tolerances, full material properties, direct from CAD. 3D printing suits complex geometry, undercuts, and internal channels that machining can't reach efficiently. Vacuum casting is the fastest route to 15–25 production-quality polyurethane units. Injection molding suits volumes above 50–100 pieces where the tooling cost amortises. If you're unsure, describe your part in the quote notes — the engineering team recommends the best fit.

**Q: Does TOP PROTO do assemblies or just individual parts?**
TOP PROTO can supply sub-assemblies — machined and sheet metal parts assembled, or over-moulded components where multiple materials are integrated. Describe your assembly requirements at quote stage. Full product assembly (mechanical + electronic) is outside the standard scope — raise the specifics and the team advises on what is feasible in-house.

### Group 4: Lead Times and Shipping

**Q: How long does it take to receive parts from TOP PROTO?**
CNC prototype orders ship in 1–5 business days from the Dongguan facility. Rush CNC orders can be machined in 1–3 business days. 3D printing ranges from 2 days (FDM) to 5–6 days (SLS). Sheet metal prototypes ship from 3 business days. Vacuum casting: 5–15 business days. Injection molding: T1 samples from 2 weeks. International shipping adds 3–7 business days depending on destination and service level. Exact lead time appears on every quote — not an estimate.

**Q: Where does TOP PROTO ship to?**
TOP PROTO ships internationally via DHL, FedEx, and UPS express services. Sea freight is available for larger and heavier orders. Countries served include USA, UK, Germany, France, Netherlands, Australia, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, UAE, and more. State your destination country at quote stage — shipping options and transit time estimates are included in the quote.

**Q: How is my order packaged for shipping?**
Each part is wrapped in VCI (Volatile Corrosion Inhibitor) film to prevent oxidation during transit. Critical surfaces receive additional foam protection. Parts are nested in EPE foam inserts sized to the part geometry — no loose parts, no machined-surface contact. Documentation — dimensional inspection report, material certificate, Certificate of Conformance — ships in the same box. Nothing to chase separately on arrival.

**Q: Can I get a rush order?**
Yes. Rush CNC prototype machining is available — parts can be ready in 1–3 business days depending on geometry and material. Rush lead times are confirmed at quote stage and reflected in the pricing. Not all materials are held in stock for same-day starts — the team confirms material availability when you request a rush.

### Group 5: Pricing

**Q: How much does CNC machining cost at TOP PROTO?**
CNC machining cost depends on material, geometry complexity, tolerance, quantity, and surface finish. A simple aluminium 6061 bracket with standard tolerances costs significantly less than a complex titanium component with ±0.005 mm positional callouts and hard anodizing. Upload your STEP file for an exact quote within 24 hours — not a range or estimate. The price on the quote is what you pay. No hidden fees for DFM review, inspection documentation, or material certifications.

**Q: Are there setup fees?**
No separate setup fee appears on the quote — setup time is included in the part price. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup time is amortised across one part rather than many. The quote shows all costs in one line per process.

**Q: Does the quote price include inspection documentation?**
Yes. Dimensional inspection report, material certifications, and Certificate of Conformance are included with every order at no additional charge. First Article Inspection and CMM reports on tight-tolerance orders are included as standard. There are no documentation fees on standard orders.
