# Frequently Asked Questions

TOP PROTO manufactures precision prototypes and low-volume production parts in Dongguan, China. The answers below give you exact specifications, lead times, and process details so you can make sourcing decisions without needing to contact anyone first.

---

## Group 1 — Quoting and Ordering

### 1. How do I get a quote from TOP PROTO?

Upload your 3D CAD file (STEP is preferred) and 2D drawing through the quote form on this website. Include your target material, surface finish, quantity, and any critical tolerances that must be held. A DFM-reviewed quote with per-part price, lead time, and manufacturability feedback arrives within 24 hours.

---

### 2. How long does it take to receive a quote?

You receive a complete quote within 24 hours of submitting your files. That quote includes a DFM (Design for Manufacturability) review, per-part pricing, and a confirmed lead time — not an estimate that changes later. Rush quoting for urgent projects is available; mention your deadline in the notes field when uploading.

---

### 3. Can I get a quote without a CAD file?

A CAD file is required to quote CNC machining, sheet metal, and injection molding accurately, because prices depend on geometry, wall thickness, and feature complexity. If your design is still in progress, send a PDF sketch or drawing and a TOP PROTO engineer will advise on the best process and flag any design concerns before you finalize the CAD. Accepted 2D formats include DXF, DWG, and PDF.

---

### 4. What file formats does TOP PROTO accept?

TOP PROTO accepts STEP, IGES, STL, DXF, DWG, and PDF. STEP (.stp / .step) is the preferred format for CNC machining and sheet metal because it carries full geometry and tolerances. STL is accepted for 3D printing; include a 2D drawing as PDF if your part has tight tolerances or critical reference dimensions that the STL file does not capture.

---

### 5. What happens after I approve my quote?

Once you approve the quote, TOP PROTO schedules production and sends you a build confirmation with your estimated ship date. Parts go through in-process quality checks against your drawing tolerances, followed by a final inspection before packaging. TOP PROTO arranges standard shipping carriers (DHL, FedEx, UPS) or sea freight based on your timeline and preference.

## Group 2 — CNC Machining

### 6. What CNC tolerances can TOP PROTO hold?

Standard CNC tolerance is ±0.01 mm per ISO 2768-m. For precision features, TOP PROTO holds ±0.005 mm. Wire EDM, used for slots, internal profiles, and hard materials, achieves ±0.002–0.005 mm. State your critical tolerances on your 2D drawing so they get checked at final inspection rather than assumed.

---

### 7. What materials can TOP PROTO machine?

Metals available for CNC machining include aluminium 6061 and 7075, stainless steel 303, 304, and 316L, titanium Grade 2 and Grade 5, brass, copper, tool steel, and steel alloy. Engineering plastics include ABS, polycarbonate (PC), PMMA (acrylic), POM (Delrin), PA (nylon), PEEK, PP, PTFE, HDPE, and Ultem. Material selection affects both machinability and lead time; titanium and PEEK parts typically require longer schedules than aluminium.

---

### 8. What is the lead time for CNC machined parts?

Standard CNC lead time is 5 business days. Rush orders ship in 1–3 business days depending on geometry and material availability. Material-specific lead times for prototypes: aluminium 3–5 business days, steel and stainless steel 5–7 business days, and titanium 7–10 business days. Include your required ship date in the quote notes so TOP PROTO can prioritize production accordingly.

---

### 9. What is the minimum order quantity for CNC machining?

There is no minimum order quantity — you can order a single part. TOP PROTO produces single prototypes through to low-volume production runs without price penalties on small quantities. This makes it practical to validate a design before committing to tooling or larger production volumes.

---

### 10. Does TOP PROTO offer 5-axis CNC machining?

Yes. 5-axis machining cuts complex geometries, undercuts, and compound angles in fewer setups, which improves positional accuracy and reduces lead time compared to multi-setup 3-axis work. Parts with features on multiple faces, complex contours, or tight positional tolerances across axes benefit most from 5-axis. Include your STEP file and a 2D drawing with GD&T callouts when quoting these parts.

---

### 11. What surface finishes are available after CNC machining?

Available post-machining surface finishes include Type II anodizing, Type III hard anodizing, powder coating, passivation, electroplating, nickel plating, bead blasting, black oxide, electropolishing, brushed finish, and polishing. As-machined parts carry a typical surface roughness of Ra 1.6 µm; specify a tighter Ra requirement on your drawing if the application demands it. Anodizing improves corrosion resistance on aluminium; passivation does the same for stainless steel.

## Group 3 — 3D Printing

### 12. What 3D printing processes does TOP PROTO offer?

TOP PROTO offers SLA (Stereolithography), SLS (Selective Laser Sintering), MJF (Multi Jet Fusion), and FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling). SLA produces the highest surface quality and fine detail, making it suited for visual models and master patterns. SLS and MJF produce functional nylon parts without support structures; FDM is cost-effective for large, lower-detail prototypes.

---

### 13. What is the lead time for 3D printed parts?

SLA and MJF parts ship in 1–3 business days. SLS parts ship in 3–5 business days. Lead time depends on part size, layer count, and post-processing requirements such as dyeing, priming, or painting. Rush 3D printing slots are available — state your deadline when requesting a quote.

---

### 14. What tolerances can SLA achieve?

SLA typically achieves ±0.1–0.2 mm on general dimensions, with accuracy dependent on part size, orientation, and geometry. Smaller parts in the 50–100 mm range hold tighter dimensional accuracy than large parts near the build platform limits. If your application requires SLA parts to fit against machined components, include a 2D drawing with critical dimensions so the build orientation and post-processing can target those features.

---

### 15. What materials are available for SLS 3D printing?

SLS at TOP PROTO uses nylon-based powders, primarily PA 12, which produces strong, functional parts with good chemical resistance and a slightly textured matte surface. SLS parts come out uncoloured grey by default but can be dyed black or other colours in post-processing. Because SLS requires no support structures, it suits complex internal channels, snap-fit assemblies, and interlocking parts that CNC tooling cannot reach.

---

### 16. When should I use 3D printing instead of CNC machining?

Use 3D printing when your part has internal channels, organic geometry, or lattice structures that CNC tooling cannot reach. 3D printing is also faster and lower cost for single iterations of design-validation models where dimensional accuracy is less critical than speed to prototype. Switch to CNC machining when your design is finalized and you need tight tolerances (±0.01 mm or better), metal mechanical properties, or a surface finish that matches the production intent.

## Group 4 — Quality and Inspection

### 17. How does TOP PROTO verify part quality?

TOP PROTO inspects parts against your drawing tolerances using calibrated CMM (coordinate measuring machine — a precision measurement device accurate to microns), calipers, and gauges during production and at final inspection. The quality team verifies and records every critical dimension called out on your 2D drawing. TOP PROTO reworks or remakes any part that does not meet the specified tolerances before shipment.

---

### 18. Does TOP PROTO provide inspection reports?

Yes. First-article inspection (FAI) reports and dimensional inspection reports are available on request. Specify "inspection report required" when placing your order so the quality team documents measurements against your drawing callouts. TOP PROTO sends reports in PDF format alongside your shipment or via email before parts leave Dongguan.

---

### 19. What is a DFM review and is it included?

DFM stands for Design for Manufacturability — an engineer checks your files for features that would increase cost, reduce quality, or cause manufacturing risk, such as wall sections too thin to machine, unsupported overhangs, or tolerances tighter than the process can reliably achieve. Every quote from TOP PROTO includes this DFM review at no additional charge. You receive specific feedback with suggested modifications before TOP PROTO cuts any material, which prevents costly remakes. The review completes within the 24-hour quote turnaround.

---

### 20. Is TOP PROTO ISO certified?

TOP PROTO holds ISO 9001:2015 certification, which covers document control, inspection procedures, corrective action, and supplier qualification. This means your order moves through a documented, audited quality management system from job creation to final inspection. If your project has specific quality documentation requirements, communicate them at the quoting stage so the quality team can confirm what is achievable and in scope.

---

### 21. Does TOP PROTO subcontract orders?

TOP PROTO manufactures parts in-house at its Dongguan facility. In-house production means quality checks, scheduling, and communication go through a single point of contact rather than through a broker or trading company. If a specialized secondary process (such as a specific plating chemistry) goes to a vetted local supplier, TOP PROTO discloses this, and that supplier operates under TOP PROTO's quality requirements.
