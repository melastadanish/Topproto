## Section 7: FAQ

**Design:** Accordion. White background.

**H2:** Quoting — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: How long does a quote take at TOP PROTO?**
Standard geometry returns instant pricing through the online system. Complex parts — tight tolerances, unusual materials, or multi-process configurations — go to engineering review and come back within 24 hours. The quote includes DFM feedback specific to your geometry, confirmed lead time, and exact pricing. If the team has questions about your drawing, they contact you directly rather than making assumptions in the quote.

**Q2: Is the DFM review genuinely included, or is it an upsell?**
The DFM review is included with every quote at no charge. It is not a paid service or an optional add-on. The engineering team reviews your geometry — wall thickness, radii, undercuts, draft angles, tolerance feasibility — and notes anything that affects cost, quality, or manufacturability. You receive the DFM notes alongside your pricing. The intent is to flag issues before you commit to production, not after first article.

**Q3: What file format gives the best quote accuracy?**
STEP (.step / .stp) gives the most accurate quote for CNC machining and sheet metal. It carries full 3D geometry — the quoting system and engineering team use it for toolpath planning and tolerance confirmation. If you have a 2D drawing with tight-tolerance callouts or GD&T, attach it alongside the STEP file. For 3D printing, STL is accepted. For laser cutting and flat profiles, DXF is ideal.

**Q4: Can I get quotes for multiple quantities in one request?**
Yes. State your quantities in the notes field — for example, "pricing for 1, 5, and 25 pieces." The engineering team provides per-unit pricing at each volume. Per-unit cost typically decreases at 5, 10, 25, and 50 pieces as setup time is amortised across more parts.

**Q5: What is the minimum order quantity?**
No minimum on CNC machining, 3D printing, or sheet metal fabrication. One part is a valid order. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces than on batches because setup time is fixed — but there is no order floor. For vacuum casting, the practical minimum is 5–10 units to justify silicone mould preparation cost. For injection molding, there is no formal MOQ but tooling cost makes very small quantities uneconomical — the engineering team will flag this and suggest alternatives at quote stage.

**Q6: How much does CNC machining cost at TOP PROTO?**
CNC machining cost depends on material, geometry complexity, tolerance, quantity, and surface finish. A simple aluminium 6061 bracket with standard ISO 2768-m tolerances costs significantly less than a complex titanium Grade 5 component with ±0.005 mm positional callouts and hard anodizing. The fastest way to get an exact number is to upload your STEP file. Pricing returns within 24 hours — no estimate ranges, no ballparks. A confirmed number.

**Q7: Do you accept NDA before sharing files?**
Yes. Contact the team to request an NDA before uploading any design files. All uploads through the quoting system are handled securely and treated as confidential. Files are not shared with third parties. For formal NDA requirements before any exchange, email the team first — the NDA is returned for signature before you upload anything.

**Q8: How does TOP PROTO compare to an instant-quote platform like Protolabs?**
Protolabs offers instant online quoting for standard geometries with fast domestic (US and EU) lead times at a significant price premium. TOP PROTO operates from Dongguan — competitive direct-manufacturer pricing, the same ±0.005 mm tolerance capability, and a DFM review that Protolabs' automated system does not provide at the same depth. For engineers outside the US and EU, or for projects requiring CNC plus 3D printing plus surface finishing from one facility, TOP PROTO is typically faster and meaningfully less expensive.
