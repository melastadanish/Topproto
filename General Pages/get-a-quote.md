## File Requirements

### Accepted File Formats

| Format | Use Case |
|---|---|
| STEP (.stp / .step) | Preferred for all 3D parts — CNC, 3D printing, vacuum casting, injection molding |
| STL (.stl) | Accepted for 3D printing only |
| IGES (.igs / .iges) | Accepted for 3D parts when STEP is unavailable |
| DXF (.dxf) | Required or preferred for sheet metal flat patterns |
| DWG (.dwg) | Accepted for sheet metal and general 2D drawings |
| PDF | Accepted for 2D engineering drawings as a supplement to a 3D file |

### What Your STEP File Should Include

Your STEP file should represent the final intended geometry — as-machined, not as-cast or as-printed. Include all holes, pockets, and threads in the model where possible. Name the file with your part number or a descriptive identifier so the engineering team can match it to your enquiry without ambiguity.

### When a 2D Drawing Is Required

Attach a 2D drawing alongside your 3D file in any of the following situations:

- Tolerances tighter than ±0.01 mm (ISO 2768-m standard) — for example, fits requiring ±0.005 mm or tighter
- Threaded features with specific tolerance classes or non-standard thread forms
- GD&T callouts (flatness, perpendicularity, true position, runout, etc.)
- Surface finish specifications beyond Ra 3.2 µm
- Any feature where the 3D model geometry alone is ambiguous

### Submitting Without a 3D File

If you have only a 2D drawing, a sketch, or a physical sample, submit what you have via the quote form and describe your part in the notes field. The engineering team reviews manual enquiries and responds within 24 hours. Quoting without a 3D file may take longer and may require a follow-up call or additional documentation before a price can be confirmed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What does my quote include?**
Your quote includes a per-part price, a DFM review, a lead time estimate, and material and process recommendations where relevant. DFM feedback flags any features that may drive up cost or affect quality before your order begins. Quotes return within 24 hours of file submission for standard enquiries — covering single parts at the same level of detail as batch orders.

**How long until I receive my quote?**
You receive your quote within 24 hours of file submission for standard enquiries. Parts with complex geometry, multiple configurations, or missing documentation may require follow-up before a final price is confirmed. The engineering team contacts you if that is the case.

**What file format should I send?**
Send a STEP file for any 3D part. For sheet metal, send a DXF or DWG of the flat pattern alongside a STEP or PDF of the folded assembly. Attach a 2D drawing in PDF format if your part has tight tolerances, GD&T, or threaded features not fully defined in the 3D model.

**What if I don't have a CAD file?**
Submit a 2D drawing, sketch, or description of your part via the quote form and include dimensions and material requirements in the notes. The team reviews manual enquiries within 24 hours. Providing a 3D STEP file accelerates quoting and reduces the chance of misinterpretation on complex geometry.

**Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No. TOP PROTO accepts orders for a single part across CNC machining, 3D printing, sheet metal fabrication, and vacuum casting. CNC prototype orders ship in as few as 1–3 business days on rush. Injection molding tooling requirements are confirmed at quote stage based on your specific geometry.

**What tolerance does my quote assume?**
Unless your drawing specifies otherwise, the quote assumes ±0.01 mm per ISO 2768-m for general dimensions. CNC machining holds ±0.005 mm on precision features when specified on your drawing. If your part requires tolerances tighter than ±0.01 mm, include a 2D drawing with explicit tolerance callouts — the quote will then reflect the correct process and inspection requirements.
