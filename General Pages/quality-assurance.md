## Section 3: The Quality Process

**Design:** Three-column. Light grey background. Pre-production | In-production | Post-production.

**H2:** Three Stages. Every Order.

**Pre-Production — Before Machining Begins**
Incoming material is verified against the material certificate — grade, heat number, and condition confirmed before any cutting starts. DFM review confirms tolerance feasibility, tool access, and setup strategy. Programming is reviewed against the drawing before the job enters the machine queue. Non-conforming raw material is rejected at goods-in — it does not enter production.

**In-Production — During Machining**
In-process dimensional checks run at defined intervals during CNC machining and sheet metal fabrication. First-off parts are measured before the full batch proceeds. Critical features — bearing bores, sealing surfaces, threaded interfaces — are checked with calibrated gauges and CMM during production. Any part outside tolerance is quarantined immediately. Production does not continue on a non-conforming setup.

**Post-Production — Before Shipment**
Final inspection confirms all drawing dimensions against measured values. Tight-tolerance orders and first articles are measured on CMM, with results logged per feature. Surface finish, cosmetic condition, thread gauging, and coating thickness checks complete the final inspection plan. Parts that pass final inspection are approved for shipment. Parts that fail go through the NCR process — they do not ship.

## Section 4: Inspection Equipment

**Design:** Three-column grid. White background.

**H2:** Measurement Equipment — Calibrated to National Standards

**CMM — Coordinate Measuring Machine**
Touch-trigger probing measures true position, perpendicularity, flatness, concentricity, and surface profile to micron accuracy — coordinate measuring machine is the reference tool for all tight-tolerance and GD&T verification. Results are logged per feature against your drawing revision and reported on the CMM inspection sheet that ships with your order.

**Contact Profilometer**
Surface roughness is measured by contact profilometer on specified surfaces. Ra values — Ra 0.8 µm, Ra 1.6 µm, Ra 3.2 µm — verified against your drawing callout, not estimated by eye. Results recorded per surface, per part.

**Thread Gauges**
Go/No-Go gauging on all threaded features — metric and imperial. Pass/fail recorded per feature, per part. Thread gauges are calibrated to relevant ISO and ANSI standards. No threaded feature ships without gauge confirmation.

**Optical Comparator**
Profile measurement for complex 2D geometries, external radii, and contoured features where CMM probe access is impractical. Particularly useful for turned profiles and sheet metal edge geometries.

**Height Gauge and Granite Surface Plate**
Calibrated manual tools for in-process step height, flatness, and datum reference verification. Used during production checks — not as the final inspection method. Final inspection uses CMM or calibrated hand measurement depending on feature type.

**Coating Thickness Gauge**
Eddy current gauges measure anodizing and plating thickness on coated parts. Results confirmed against drawing specification. Coating process is not signed off until thickness is within the specified range.

## Section 5: Quality Documentation

**Design:** Six-card grid. Light grey background.

**H2:** What Ships with Every Order

**Dimensional Inspection Report**
All critical dimensions measured against drawing nominal and tolerance. Measured value, nominal, tolerance, and pass/fail status per feature — referenced to your drawing revision. Included on every order. No request required.

**Material Certificate**
Mill certificate confirming alloy grade, heat number, temper designation, and mechanical properties for every batch of raw material. Traceable to the specific parts in your order — not a generic batch reference. Included on every order.

**Certificate of Conformance**
Written declaration that the parts in the shipment conform to your drawing, specification, and order requirements. Signed by the quality team before shipment approval. Included on every order.

**CMM Inspection Report**
Coordinate measuring machine output for geometric tolerances — true position, perpendicularity, flatness, concentricity. Provided as standard on all tight-tolerance and first-article orders. Available on request for standard tolerance work.

**First Article Inspection Report (FAI)**
Full dimensional layout on the first piece of any new production run. Establishes the baseline for all subsequent batches. Available in AS9102 (aerospace) or AIAG PPAP (automotive) format on request. Cpk data available for ongoing production programmes.

**RoHS and Compliance Declarations**
Declarations of conformity for material and substance compliance. Available for relevant markets on request. Raise compliance requirements at quote stage so the team can confirm material and process compatibility.

## Section 8: Non-Conformance Handling

**Design:** Three-step process. Light grey background.

**H2:** What Happens When a Part Falls Outside Tolerance

Non-conforming parts are not shipped to meet a deadline. They are quarantined immediately and dispositioned through a formal NCR — Non-Conformance Report — process.

**Step 1 — Quarantine**
Any part that fails in-process or final inspection is quarantined and tagged. It does not proceed to the next stage. The engineering team notifies the quality manager and raises an NCR.

**Step 2 — Disposition**
Disposition options are rework (bring back into tolerance), replacement (remake the part), or concession (accept with documented customer approval). All dispositions are documented. You are notified of any disposition that affects your delivery timeline before it becomes a problem — not after.

**Step 3 — Root Cause and Corrective Action**
Every NCR triggers a root cause investigation. The corrective action is documented, implemented, and verified before the job closes. On repeat production programmes, statistical process control data identifies trends before they result in non-conformances.

## Section 9: Packaging and Shipping

**Design:** Three-step horizontal. White background.

**H2:** Parts Arrive in the Same Condition They Left

**Part Protection**
Each part is wrapped in VCI — Volatile Corrosion Inhibitor — film. VCI prevents oxidation on aluminium, steel, and copper alloys during transit. Critical machined surfaces receive additional foam padding to prevent contact damage.

**Transit Packaging**
Parts are nested in EPE foam inserts sized to the part geometry. No loose parts. No contact between machined surfaces in the same box. Carton or wooden case selected based on part weight, geometry, and fragility. Fragile or high-value parts receive double-box packaging.

**Documentation in Every Box**
Dimensional inspection report, material certificate, Certificate of Conformance, and packing list ship inside the box. Nothing to chase on arrival. Everything needed to receive, inspect, and approve the parts is included in the shipment.

## Section 10: FAQ

**Design:** Accordion. White background.

**H2:** Quality Assurance — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Do you provide CMM inspection reports as standard?**
CMM reports are standard on all tight-tolerance orders and first articles. Standard tolerance orders include a dimensional inspection report from calibrated hand measurement — CMM is available on request for any order. The CMM report shows measured values, nominal dimensions, tolerances, and pass/fail status per feature — referenced to your specific drawing revision, not a generic template. Every order ships with this documentation regardless of process or material.

**Q2: What happens if a part fails inspection at TOP PROTO?**
Non-conforming parts are quarantined immediately and processed through a formal NCR — Non-Conformance Report — procedure. They do not ship to meet a deadline. Disposition is rework, replacement, or concession — all documented. You are notified of any disposition that affects your delivery timeline before it becomes an issue. Root cause investigation closes every NCR, and the corrective action is tracked through to verification.

**Q3: Can you supply material certificates traceable to heat number?**
Yes. Material certificates confirming alloy grade, heat number, temper designation, and mechanical properties ship with every order. The certificate traces to the specific raw stock used on your job — not to a generic batch. For aerospace and medical applications where full material traceability is a contractual requirement, confirm this at quote stage so the team allocates certified stock to your job from the start.

**Q4: Is TOP PROTO ISO 9001:2015 certified?**
Yes. TOP PROTO holds ISO 9001:2015 certification, independently audited annually by an accredited third-party body. The certification covers the full production scope: CNC machining, 3D printing, sheet metal fabrication, casting, wire EDM, and surface finishing. It is not a self-declaration. The certificate is available on request.

**Q5: What tolerances can TOP PROTO hold and verify?**
Standard CNC work follows ISO 2768-m — ±0.01 mm on linear dimensions. Precision features hold to ±0.005 mm, confirmed by CMM. Wire EDM reaches ±0.002–0.005 mm on 2D profiles. Sheet metal follows ISO 2768-c — ±0.2 mm general tolerance. Achievable tolerance depends on geometry, material, and feature size. If a callout is at the limit of what is practical, the engineering team flags it during DFM review — before production starts.

**Q6: Do you perform First Article Inspection?**
Yes. FAI is available on all production orders and performed as standard on first runs for new parts. The FAI establishes the dimensional baseline for subsequent batches. Reports format to AS9102 (aerospace) or AIAG PPAP (automotive) on request. Cpk data is available for production programmes where process capability monitoring is a customer requirement.

**Q7: How does TOP PROTO compare to a supplier without ISO certification?**
An uncertified supplier relies on individual inspector judgement and has no formal system for handling non-conformances, tracking corrective actions, or verifying that fixes actually work. TOP PROTO's ISO 9001:2015 certified QMS means every step — incoming material, in-process checks, final inspection, NCR handling, and shipping release — follows a documented procedure, audited by an external body annually. The practical difference: consistently documented parts, formal accountability when something goes wrong, and a paper trail that supports your own quality system.

**Q8: What documentation is included with every order at no charge?**
Every order includes: dimensional inspection report, material certificate (traceable to heat number), and Certificate of Conformance. Tight-tolerance and first-article orders include CMM reports as standard. FAI reports are available on request for production orders. No documentation fee applies to any of these. If your project requires additional documentation — PPAP, AS9102 FAI, compliance declarations — raise it at quote stage.
