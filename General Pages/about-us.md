## What TOP PROTO Manufactures

Your project can require any combination of processes across development stages. TOP PROTO covers eight manufacturing methods under one roof, so your file goes to the right process without redirection.

**CNC Machining**
Multi-axis CNC milling and turning hold tolerances to ±0.005 mm. Standard delivery is 5 business days; rush orders ship in 1–3 business days.

**3D Printing — SLA**
Stereolithography produces smooth-surface prototypes with fine feature detail. SLA suits visual models and functional parts requiring tight dimensional accuracy before tooling.

**3D Printing — SLS**
Selective laser sintering fuses nylon powder into durable, uncoated parts without support structures. SLS is suited for complex geometries and functional end-use testing.

**3D Printing — MJF**
Multi Jet Fusion produces isotropic nylon parts with consistent mechanical properties throughout the cross-section. MJF suits short-run functional parts faster than injection molding.

**3D Printing — FDM**
Fused deposition modeling builds thermoplastic parts layer by layer for low-cost concept models and jigs. FDM accepts a wide range of engineering-grade materials including ABS, PLA, and PETG.

**Sheet Metal Fabrication**
Laser cutting, bending, and welding convert your DXF or DWG files into sheet metal enclosures, brackets, and structural components. Standard tolerance follows ISO 2768-m at ±0.01 mm.

**Vacuum Casting**
Silicone molds replicate master patterns to produce polyurethane parts that match injection-molded aesthetics and properties. Vacuum casting delivers small batches at a fraction of hard-tooling cost.

**Injection Molding**
Short-run injection molding turns your 3D model into repeatable thermoplastic parts. Your mold file and material selection determine cycle time and per-part cost.

**Wire EDM**
Wire electrical discharge machining cuts hard metals and complex profiles where conventional milling cannot reach. Wire EDM holds tight tolerances on hardened tool steels, carbides, and exotic alloys.

## How Your Order Moves Through the Facility

Every order follows the same four-stage sequence — no shortcuts, no hand-waving.

**1. Quote and DFM Review — Within 24 Hours**
Submit your STEP, STL, DXF, DWG, IGES, or PDF file. An engineer checks manufacturability, flags wall thickness issues, undercuts, or tolerance conflicts, and returns a costed quote within 24 hours. You get the DFM feedback whether you place the order or not.

**2. Production**
Your job enters the schedule after you approve the quote. CNC work starts at 5-business-day standard lead time, with 1–3 business day rush slots available. Other processes carry their own lead times, stated in your quote.

**3. Inspection**
Engineers run dimensional inspection on your parts before packaging. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — verifies critical dimensions against your drawing to micron-level accuracy. Any part that fails the dimensional check is flagged before shipment.

**4. Shipping**
Parts leave Dongguan via DHL, FedEx, UPS, or sea freight based on your delivery requirement and budget. Tracking details go to you as soon as the carrier scans the package.
