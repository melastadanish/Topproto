## Section 8: FAQ

**Design:** Accordion. White background.

**H2:** Frequently Asked Questions About TOP PROTO

**Q1: Where is TOP PROTO located and does that affect lead time?**
TOP PROTO manufactures in Dongguan, China — a dense manufacturing corridor with material suppliers, tooling houses, and specialist process vendors within close proximity. Prototype CNC parts ship from the Dongguan facility in 1–5 business days. International shipping to North America, Europe, and Asia adds 3–7 days depending on the freight service selected. DHL, FedEx, and UPS express options are available.

**Q2: Is TOP PROTO a manufacturer or a marketplace?**
TOP PROTO is a direct manufacturer. Your parts are quoted, programmed, machined, inspected, and shipped by one team in one facility. There is no routing to third-party shops on standard orders. That is a different model from marketplace platforms like Xometry, which aggregate orders across hundreds of shops with variable quality controls. One facility. One ISO 9001:2015 certified quality system. One accountable team.

**Q3: What manufacturing processes does TOP PROTO run in-house?**
Eight processes run in-house: CNC machining (milling, turning, 5-axis), SLA/SLS/MJF/FDM 3D printing, sheet metal fabrication (laser cutting, bending, welding), vacuum casting, injection molding, wire EDM, rapid prototyping, and surface finishing. All in one Dongguan facility — one shipment regardless of which combination your project requires.

**Q4: What quality documentation ships with every order?**
Every order includes a dimensional inspection report, material certifications traceable to heat and lot number, and a Certificate of Conformance. First Article Inspection Reports formatted to AS9102 or AIAG PPAP are available on request. No additional charge for standard inspection documentation.

**Q5: What is the minimum order quantity?**
No minimum on CNC machining, 3D printing, or sheet metal — a single part is a valid order. Per-unit cost on single pieces is higher than batch pricing because setup time is fixed regardless of quantity, but there is no order floor. Vacuum casting has a practical minimum of 5–10 units to justify silicone mould preparation. Injection molding has no formal MOQ, but tooling cost makes very small quantities uneconomical — raise this at quote stage and the team will recommend the right process.

**Q6: What tolerances can TOP PROTO hold?**
Standard CNC work follows ISO 2768-m — ±0.01 mm on linear dimensions. Precision features hold to ±0.005 mm. Wire EDM reaches ±0.002–0.005 mm on 2D profiles. 3D printing tolerances range from ±0.1 mm (SLA) to ±0.3 mm (FDM and SLS) depending on process. Every tight-tolerance order ships with a dimensional inspection report confirming actual measured values against nominal.

**Q7: How much does manufacturing at TOP PROTO cost?**
Cost depends on process, material, geometry, quantity, and tolerance. A simple aluminium bracket in 6061 costs considerably less than a complex titanium part with tight positional tolerances. Upload your STEP file for an exact quote within 24 hours — DFM notes included. No fees for the DFM review or inspection documentation. The quoted price is what you pay.

**Q8: How does TOP PROTO compare to a local CNC shop?**
A local shop offers proximity — easier to visit, no international shipping lead time. TOP PROTO offers a broader process range under one ISO 9001:2015 certified quality system, direct-manufacturer pricing from Dongguan, and documented inspection output that most local prototype shops don't match. For projects needing CNC plus 3D printing plus surface finishing, sourcing everything from one certified facility is simpler and faster than managing multiple separate local vendors.
