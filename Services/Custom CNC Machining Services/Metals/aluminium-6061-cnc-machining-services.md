## Section 2: Why 6061 for CNC Machining

**Design:** Two-column. White background. Text left, image right.

**H2:** Aluminium 6061 — Why It's the Default for CNC Work

Aluminium 6061-T6 is the most commonly specified aluminium for CNC machined parts. The reasons are practical: it machines fast, holds tight tolerances without specialist tooling, anodizes well, and is available in every common stock form. For engineers who need structural parts, housings, brackets, and fixtures back in days rather than weeks, 6061-T6 is the starting point.

The machinability is the first reason engineers reach for it. 6061 produces short, manageable chips at high cutting speeds. Feed rates can be pushed aggressively without the work-hardening risk that comes with stainless steel, and without the heat management demands of titanium. That speed directly reduces cycle time — and cost on any order above a handful of pieces.

Weldability sets 6061 apart from 7075. If a design needs post-machine welding — joining sections, attaching brackets, closing a housing — 6061 is the correct alloy. 7075 cannot be welded without crack risk. This is a decision point that matters on assembly designs.

The finish behaviour is the third consideration. 6061-T6 anodizes with good colour consistency. Type II anodize for corrosion protection and colour. Type III hardcoat for wear interfaces. The alloy's silicon and magnesium content produces a stable, uniform anodic layer — not the colour variation that affects some higher-zinc alloys like 7075 on colour anodizing.

## Section 3: Aluminium 6061 Properties and Machinability

**Design:** Two-column. Light grey background. Properties table left, machinability notes right.

**H2:** 6061 Mechanical Properties and Machining Behaviour

**Properties table — 6061-T6:**

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Tensile Strength | 310 MPa |
| Yield Strength (0.2%) | 276 MPa |
| Elongation | 12% |
| Hardness | 95 HRB (Brinell) |
| Density | 2.70 g/cm³ |
| Thermal Conductivity | 167 W/m·K |
| Machinability Rating | Excellent — ~50% relative to brass C360 |
| Weldability | Good (MIG, TIG) |

**Machinability notes:**

6061-T6 cuts cleanly at high spindle speeds with uncoated or TiN-coated carbide tooling. Chip control is straightforward — short chips break well and clear the cut without re-cutting damage. Feed rates 2–3× higher than stainless steel are typical, which keeps cycle times low.

The one area that requires attention on 6061 is thin-wall geometry. Aluminium's low modulus means thin walls deflect during cutting — you can hear the chatter if the setup is wrong. Fixturing strategy and tool engagement are managed per drawing: thin features are identified at DFM review and the machining sequence adjusted before cutting starts.

6061-T651 (stress-relieved by stretching) is specified over standard T6 plate when heavy stock removal is involved. Standard 6061 plate carries rolling residual stress — remove material asymmetrically and the part can move after unclamping. T651 minimises this. For tooling plates and precision flat parts where post-machine stability matters, specify T651 or MIC-6.

## Section 4: 6061 Tempers

**Design:** Full-width table. White background.

**H2:** Aluminium 6061 Tempers — Choosing the Right Condition

| Temper | Condition | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|
| **6061-T6** | Solution heat treated and artificially aged | Standard machining — best combination of strength and machinability for most applications |
| **6061-T651** | T6 + stress-relieved by stretching | Precision flat parts and plates where dimensional stability after heavy stock removal is required |
| **6061-O** | Annealed — soft condition | Forming and bending applications; not used for machined structural parts |
| **6061-T4** | Solution heat treated — natural age | Lower strength than T6; used where post-forming heat treatment is planned |

T6 is the default for CNC machining. Specify T651 when ordering plate for precision flat parts or fixtures. Confirm the temper at quote stage — both are stocked and switching between them adds no lead time.

## Section 6: Surface Finishes for Aluminium 6061

**Design:** Full-width table. Light grey background.

**H2:** Surface Finishes for 6061 Parts

| Finish | Dimensional Impact | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| As Machined | None | Internal components, tooling, structural parts |
| Bead Blasted | Negligible | Cosmetic uniformity before anodizing |
| Anodize Type II | +5–25 µm per side | Corrosion protection, colour (clear, black, blue, red, gold) |
| Anodize Type III (Hardcoat) | +25–50 µm per side | Wear interfaces, aggressive environments. HV 400–600. |
| Chromate Conversion (Alodine) | Negligible | Electrical conductivity required — MIL-DTL-5541 |
| Powder Coat | +50–100 µm per side | Colour, impact resistance — mask threads and precision features |
| Polishing | Removes minor surface layer | Ra down to 0.05 µm for optical or cosmetic applications |
| Brushed Finish | Negligible | Directional satin texture — instrument panels, consumer parts |

**Anodizing and tight-tolerance features:** Type II adds 5–25 µm per side; Type III adds 25–50 µm per side. A bore machined to H7 tolerance and then anodized without adjustment is no longer H7. Precision-fit features are identified at quote stage and the approach — pre-machine undersized, mask, or post-coat machine — agreed before the job starts.

## Section 7: Design Guidelines for Aluminium 6061

**Design:** Table. White background.

**H2:** Design Guidelines for 6061 CNC Machining

| Feature | Recommendation | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Min wall thickness | 0.8 mm structural / 1.0 mm recommended | Thinner walls deflect during cutting and produce dimensional drift |
| Internal corner radius | Min 1/3 of cavity depth | Tool diameter governs minimum achievable radius |
| Hole depth | Max 10× diameter (standard drill) | Deep holes require peck drilling — flag depths >10D at quote stage |
| Thread engagement | Min 1.5× diameter | 6061 is softer than steel — under-specified engagement strips under load |
| Anodize allowance on precision fits | Pre-machine offset or mask | Type III adds 25–50 µm per side — must be accounted for before coating |
| Plate temper for flat parts | T651 or MIC-6 | Standard 6061-T6 plate can move after heavy asymmetric stock removal |
| Rib aspect ratio | ≤8:1 (height:thickness) | Tall thin ribs chatter and produce tolerance drift |

## Section 10: FAQ

**Design:** Accordion. White background.

**H2:** Aluminium 6061 CNC Machining — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerance can you hold on aluminium 6061 CNC machined parts?**
Standard aluminium 6061 CNC work follows ISO 2768-m — ±0.01 mm on linear dimensions for most features. Precision features, bearing bores, sealing faces, and positional callouts hold to ±0.005 mm, confirmed by CMM and reported per feature against your drawing revision. Ra 0.4 µm surface finish is achievable on specified surfaces through controlled machining parameters. Tolerance achievability on any specific feature depends on geometry, wall section, and tool access — the engineering team assesses this during DFM review and flags any callout at the edge of practical capability before cutting starts.

**Q2: What is the difference between 6061-T6 and 6061-T651?**
Both are the same alloy composition. The temper designation indicates the post-processing condition. T6 is solution heat treated and artificially aged — standard temper for most machined parts. T651 adds a stress-relief step by stretching: the plate is pulled in tension after heat treatment to relieve rolling residual stresses. That stress relief matters when heavy material is removed asymmetrically. Standard 6061-T6 plate can spring — the internal stress releases as you machine away material and the part lifts off the fixture. T651 doesn't do that. For precision flat parts, tooling plates, and fixtures where post-machine stability is a requirement, T651 is the correct specification.

**Q3: What surface finishes work best on aluminium 6061?**
6061 anodizes with good colour consistency — it's a well-behaved alloy for both Type II (5–25 µm, corrosion and colour) and Type III hardcoat (25–50 µm, wear resistance, HV 400–600). For electrical-conductivity-critical applications, chromate conversion (Alodine, MIL-DTL-5541) is correct — anodizing is electrically insulating. For cosmetic uniformity, bead blast before anodizing to eliminate toolmarks and produce a consistent surface texture across the batch. Powder coating works on 6061 but adds 50–100 µm per side — mask threads and precision bores before application.

**Q4: How does anodizing affect the dimensions of 6061 parts?**
Type II anodize adds approximately 5–25 µm per side; Type III hardcoat adds 25–50 µm per side. On a bore machined to H7 fit tolerance, either is enough to push the feature out of specification without adjustment. TOP PROTO reviews anodizing allowances at quote stage for every 6061 job involving tight-fit features. The approach — machine undersized by the coating allowance, mask the feature during anodizing, or machine after coating — is agreed before production starts, not discovered at inspection.

**Q5: What is the minimum order quantity for aluminium 6061 CNC machining?**
No minimum. One 6061 part is a valid order. Single prototypes, small development batches, and repeat production runs receive the same material verification, dimensional inspection, and shipping documentation. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup time is fixed regardless of quantity, but there is no order floor. If you need pricing at multiple quantities — 1, 5, and 25 pieces — state them in the quote notes.

**Q6: What file formats does TOP PROTO accept for 6061 CNC quotes?**
STEP (.step / .stp) is the preferred format — it carries full 3D geometry and is what the engineering team uses for toolpath planning and DFM review. IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), CATIA (.CATPart), Parasolid (.x_t), DXF, DWG, and PDF drawings are also accepted. Attach your 2D drawing alongside the STEP file when tight-tolerance callouts or GD&T requirements appear on the drawing — the 2D drawing becomes the tolerance authority.

**Q7: How much does aluminium 6061 CNC machining cost?**
Cost depends on geometry complexity, tolerance class, quantity, surface finish, and whether anodizing is required. A simple 6061-T6 bracket with ISO 2768-m tolerances and bead blast finish costs significantly less than a complex housing with ±0.005 mm positional callouts and hardcoat anodizing. Upload your STEP file for pricing within 24 hours — not an estimate range. DFM review is included with the quote at no additional charge. No setup fee appears separately on the quote.
