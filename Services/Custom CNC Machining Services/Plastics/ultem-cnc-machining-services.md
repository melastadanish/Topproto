## Section 2: Why Ultem for CNC Machining

**H2:** Ultem — High Temperature, Flame Retardance, and Autoclave Compatibility

Ultem (SABIC's trade name for polyetherimide, PEI) occupies the performance tier between standard engineering plastics like PEEK's near-metal-performance upper tier and general plastics like polycarbonate. Ultem's combination of high continuous service temperature (170°C), inherent UL94 V-0 flame retardance without flame-retardant additives, excellent electrical insulation properties, and autoclave sterilisation compatibility makes it the specification for aerospace interiors, medical device housings, and electrical assemblies that must survive high temperature and have a low flame propagation requirement.

The 170°C continuous service temperature places Ultem above most engineering plastics — above polycarbonate (120°C), ABS (80–90°C), and nylon PA6 in unfilled form — but below PEEK (250°C). For applications where service temperature requires something above polycarbonate and nylon but the cost of PEEK is difficult to justify, Ultem is the engineering solution. The inherent flame retardance is particularly significant: Ultem achieves UL94 V-0 without added flame-retardant compounds that can affect other properties or raise regulatory concerns — the PEI chemistry is inherently flame-retardant.

Autoclave compatibility at 134°C steam sterilisation is achieved with correct grade selection. Ultem 1010 is the food-contact and steam-sterilisation grade — it withstands repeated autoclave cycles and is FDA-compliant for food contact and some medical device applications. This property, combined with high temperature resistance and flame retardance, makes Ultem the material specified for reusable medical device housings and surgical tray components that must survive both high operating temperatures and repeated sterilisation.

Machinability is moderate to challenging. Ultem is stiffer and more abrasive on tooling than standard engineering plastics — though less demanding than glass-filled PEEK. Sharp tools and correct feeds are required. Ultem is amorphous (not semi-crystalline like PEEK or Delrin), which means no annealing is required but also means it does not have the same degree of self-lubrication as crystalline polymers. Chips are controlled and predictable.

## Section 7: Applications and Industries

**H2:** Where Ultem CNC Machined Parts Are Used

**Aerospace Interiors** — Cabin interior components, seat hardware, panel brackets, and ducting clips where UL94 V-0 flame performance is mandated by FAR 25.853 and equivalent regulations. Ultem's inherent flame retardance (no additives) and high temperature resistance are the specification properties. Ultem 2300 for load-bearing structural aerospace components.

**Medical Device Housings** — Reusable surgical instrument housings, tray components, and device enclosures that undergo repeated autoclave sterilisation. Ultem 1010 withstands 134°C steam under pressure for repeated cycles without dimensional degradation. FDA compliance supports documentation requirements for medical devices.

**Electrical and Electronic Components** — Connectors, insulators, and housings in high-temperature electrical assemblies. Ultem's dielectric properties are stable at elevated temperature — useful in motor housings, power electronics packaging, and high-frequency electrical insulators.

**Food Processing Equipment** — Housings, fixtures, and components in food processing equipment requiring both high temperature resistance (steam cleaning) and FDA food-contact compliance. Ultem 1010 satisfies both requirements in a single material.

**Semiconductor and Process Equipment** — Components in semiconductor process tools that require chemical resistance, high temperature stability, and dimensional precision. Ultem's resistance to the moderate chemical environments in wafer handling equipment (less aggressive than PEEK semiconductor applications) makes it a cost-effective intermediate option.

**Defence and Industrial Equipment** — Structural housings and components where high temperature and inherent flame retardance are required without the cost of PEEK. Ultem is widely used in defence electronics and industrial control equipment.

## Section 9: FAQ

**H2:** Ultem CNC Machining — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerance can you hold on Ultem CNC machined parts?**
Standard Ultem CNC work achieves ±0.05 mm on most features. Precision bores and positional callouts hold ±0.025 mm with appropriate fixturing. Ultem is amorphous — it does not have the residual crystallisation stress of semi-crystalline polymers like PEEK, which means annealing before final machining is generally not required. Dimensional stability in service is good — low moisture absorption (1.25%) and good creep resistance at elevated temperature relative to lower-performance engineering plastics. Confirm tolerance requirements at quote stage.

**Q2: What is the difference between Ultem 1010 and Ultem 2300?**
Ultem 1010 is unfilled polyetherimide — it provides FDA food-contact compliance, autoclave sterilisation compatibility (134°C steam), maximum chemical resistance, and UL94 V-0 flame retardance. It is the standard grade for medical device and food-contact applications. Ultem 2300 is 30% glass-fibre-filled PEI — higher stiffness (170 MPa tensile vs 105 MPa for 1010), lower creep under load, and slightly better dimensional stability, but at the cost of FDA food-contact clearance and glass-fibre abrasion on cutting tools. Ultem 2300 is used for structural aerospace components and load-bearing housings where stiffness is the primary requirement. Both grades share the same UL94 V-0 flame rating. Specify the grade on your drawing — if unsure which suits your application, the engineering team will advise at DFM stage.

**Q3: Is Ultem compatible with autoclave sterilisation?**
Ultem 1010 (unfilled grade) withstands repeated autoclave sterilisation cycles at 134°C steam under pressure. The amorphous PEI structure and high HDT (217°C for Ultem 1010) give it margin above autoclave temperatures that semi-crystalline polymers with lower HDTs do not have. For reusable surgical instrument housings and medical device components requiring repeated sterilisation cycles, Ultem 1010 is a cost-effective alternative to PEEK where PEEK's full temperature and chemical resistance are not required. Confirm the specific sterilisation method and cycle parameters at quote stage — EtO and gamma radiation are also compatible with Ultem 1010.

**Q4: What surface finish is achievable on Ultem CNC machined parts?**
As-machined Ultem achieves Ra 1.6 µm on most surfaces — the standard finish for medical device housings, aerospace brackets, and electrical components. Ra 0.8 µm is achievable with fine machining parameters. Bead blasting provides cosmetic uniformity and is standard for medical device housings requiring a consistent matte finish. Polishing to Ra 0.4 µm is achievable for surfaces requiring smooth cleanability. Ultem does not accept most adhesives or coatings without surface activation; for bonding Ultem assemblies, mechanical fasteners, snap fits, or welding techniques are standard.

**Q5: What is the minimum order quantity for Ultem CNC machining?**
No minimum. One Ultem part is a valid order. Material verification, dimensional inspection, and documentation are the same standard regardless of quantity. Single prototypes and development samples are routine.

**Q6: What file formats does TOP PROTO accept for Ultem CNC quotes?**
STEP (.step / .stp) preferred. Also IGES, SolidWorks, CATIA, Parasolid, DXF, DWG, and PDF drawings. Attach 2D drawing when tolerance callouts, grade specifications, or FDA/autoclave compliance requirements appear. Note the intended service environment (temperature, sterilisation method, chemical exposure) in the quote request to allow correct grade recommendation.

**Q7: How much does Ultem CNC machining cost?**
Ultem is a premium engineering plastic — higher material cost than Delrin, nylon, or polycarbonate, but lower than PEEK. Ultem 1010 and 2300 material prices are similar; Ultem 2300 machines with higher tool wear due to glass fibre abrasion. Compared to PEEK, Ultem provides a cost-effective intermediate option where PEEK's 250°C service temperature and wider chemical resistance are not required. Upload your STEP file for exact pricing within 24 hours. DFM review is included at no additional charge.

**Q8: How does Ultem compare to PEEK for high-temperature engineering plastic applications?**
Ultem (PEI) and PEEK (polyether ether ketone) both target high-performance engineering plastic applications but at different performance levels and costs. PEEK has a continuous service temperature of 250°C vs Ultem's 170°C — PEEK is the specification when the application exceeds 170°C. PEEK has better chemical resistance — it resists concentrated acids and bases that attack Ultem. PEEK is also biocompatible per ISO 10993 in unfilled form and preferred for implant-adjacent medical applications. Ultem costs significantly less than PEEK and has inherent UL94 V-0 flame retardance — an advantage in aerospace interiors where PEEK requires additives or other engineering approaches for flame compliance. Choose Ultem when service temperature is below 170°C, flame retardance is required, and cost relative to PEEK matters. Choose PEEK when service temperature exceeds 170°C, chemical environment is aggressive, or biocompatibility for extended body contact is required.
