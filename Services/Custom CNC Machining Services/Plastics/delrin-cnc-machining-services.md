## Section 2: Why Delrin for CNC Machining

**H2:** Delrin — The Precision Engineering Plastic

Delrin (DuPont's trade name for acetal homopolymer, POM-H) is the default engineering plastic for precision mechanical components. Its combination of stiffness, low friction, dimensional stability, and machinability covers more plastic engineering applications than any other single thermoplastic. When the part needs to hold tight dimensions, run against a mating surface without lubrication, and not absorb water and drift out of tolerance — Delrin is the starting specification.

Dimensional stability is the key property. Delrin absorbs less than 0.2% moisture — versus 1.0–1.5% for nylon. That means a Delrin bore machined to H7 fit stays at H7 fit in humid service; a nylon bore grows. For precision gears, pump components, and bearing housings where the fit must be consistent across varying humidity and temperature, Delrin's stability is not a marginal improvement — it is the difference between a part that works and one that doesn't.

Machinability is excellent. Delrin cuts cleanly, produces good surface finish, holds tolerances well, and is significantly less demanding on tooling than filled PEEK or glass-filled nylon. It is the plastic that most closely resembles machining a soft non-ferrous metal in terms of predictability and cycle time.

The limits of Delrin are also specific. It cannot be welded. It is not compatible with strong acids or bases. It does not perform at temperatures above 100°C. For applications within those limits, Delrin is the correct and most economical precision plastic specification.

## Section 5: Applications and Industries

**H2:** Where Delrin CNC Machined Parts Are Used

**Precision Gears and Drive Components** — Gears, rack sections, worm gears, and cam profiles. Delrin's low friction and dimensional stability make it the standard plastic gear material. Runs quietly against steel or Delrin mating gears.

**Bushings and Bearings** — Sleeve bushings, thrust washers, and plain bearing components in low-to-medium load, dry-running applications. Self-lubricating. No corrosion in water.

**Fluid Handling** — Valve bodies, pump rotors, impellers, and manifold components in water, fuel, and mild chemical service. Chemical resistance adequate for most common industrial fluids.

**Medical Devices** — Housing components, fixture frames, and structural hardware for non-implant medical equipment. Low moisture absorption is an advantage in sterilised environments.

**Robotics and Automation** — Guide rails, carriage components, end-effector housings, and sensor brackets. Light weight and machinability make Delrin a cost-effective structural plastic for robotic systems.

**Electronics and Instrumentation** — Insulating components, connector housings, and non-conductive structural parts in electrical assemblies.

## Section 7: FAQ

**H2:** Delrin CNC Machining — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerance can you hold on Delrin CNC machined parts?**
Standard Delrin work achieves ±0.05 mm on most features. Precision bores achieve ±0.025 mm with appropriate fixturing. Delrin's moisture absorption is below 0.2% — one of the lowest of any engineering thermoplastic — which means dimensional change in service from humidity is minimal compared to nylon or ABS. For precision fits, Delrin is the most dimensionally reliable common engineering plastic.

**Q2: What is the difference between Delrin and acetal copolymer (POM-C)?**
Delrin is the trade name for DuPont's acetal homopolymer (POM-H). Acetal copolymer (POM-C, sold under names like Celcon and Hostaform) has a similar property profile but with slightly better chemical resistance to alkaline environments and slightly lower strength. POM-H (Delrin) has higher crystallinity, better stiffness, and better fatigue resistance. For most CNC machining applications, the choice between the two is minor — POM-H is the default for precision mechanical components; POM-C for chemical environments with alkali exposure.

**Q3: Can Delrin be used in food contact applications?**
Natural (white) Delrin POM-H is certified for food contact per FDA 21 CFR 177.2470 and meets EU food contact regulations in most standard grades. It is used for food processing equipment components, conveyor components, and food-contact valve parts. Confirm the specific grade certification at quote stage — food contact grades have specific certificate documentation required.

**Q4: What surface finish is achievable on Delrin CNC parts?**
As-machined Ra 1.6 µm is standard on Delrin. Ra 0.8 µm is achievable with fine machining parameters. Polishing achieves Ra 0.2–0.4 µm for precision bearing surfaces. Delrin does not accept most adhesives or paints without surface activation — for bonding, mechanical fasteners, snap fits, or press fits are the standard design approach for Delrin assemblies.

**Q5: What is the minimum order quantity for Delrin CNC machining?**
No minimum. One Delrin part is a valid order. Material verification, dimensional inspection, and documentation are the same standard regardless of quantity.

**Q6: What file formats does TOP PROTO accept for Delrin CNC quotes?**
STEP (.step / .stp) preferred. Also IGES, SolidWorks, CATIA, Parasolid, DXF, DWG, and PDF drawings. Attach 2D drawing when tolerance callouts or fit class specifications appear.

**Q7: How much does Delrin CNC machining cost?**
Delrin is one of the most cost-effective precision plastic machining options — lower material cost than PEEK or Ultem, and excellent machinability keeps cycle times low. A standard Delrin gear or bushing costs significantly less than equivalent PEEK or aluminium work. Upload your STEP file for exact pricing within 24 hours.

**Q8: How does Delrin compare to nylon for CNC machined precision parts?**
Delrin has significantly lower moisture absorption than nylon (0.2% vs 1.0–1.5%) — the practical effect is that Delrin maintains tighter fits in humid environments. Nylon has better impact resistance and is self-lubricating in ways that make it better for gear applications with occasional impact loading. Delrin has better stiffness and dimensional predictability for precision bores and close fits. Choose Delrin for precision fits and gears where dimensional stability in varying humidity is critical. Choose nylon for impact-resistant structural parts and applications benefiting from nylon's higher elongation before fracture.
