## Section 2: What Precision Machining Covers

**Design:** Two-column. White background. Text left, four-stat callouts right.

**H2:** One Facility. Four CNC Processes. One Quality Standard.

Precision machining is subtractive manufacturing — cutting tools remove material from a solid workpiece to produce finished geometry. CNC control means the machine follows a toolpath derived from your CAD file, reproducing features accurately across every part in the run. The machined surface is the final surface. No tooling, no curing time, no layer interfaces.

TOP PROTO operates CNC milling, CNC turning, 5-axis machining, and surface finishing from a single facility in Dongguan. All four processes run under the same ISO 9001:2015 quality management system. One order covers all the processes your part requires — no separate suppliers, no handoffs between facilities.

Precision machining is the standard route for tight-tolerance metal and plastic components. Single prototypes or batches of 50 — no minimum order. Parts are dimensionally inspected against your drawing before shipment. Material certifications trace to heat number.

## Section 7: Quality Documentation

**Design:** Six-card grid. White background.

**H2:** What Ships with Every Precision Machining Order

**Dimensional Inspection Report**
All critical dimensions measured against drawing nominal and tolerance. Measured value, nominal, tolerance, and pass/fail status per feature — referenced to your drawing revision. Included on every order.

**Material Certificate**
Mill certificate confirming alloy grade, heat number, temper, and mechanical properties for every batch of raw material. Traceable to the specific parts in your order.

**Certificate of Conformance**
Written declaration that the parts in the shipment conform to your drawing, specification, and order requirements. Signed before shipment approval.

**CMM Inspection Report**
Coordinate measuring machine output for geometric tolerances — true position, perpendicularity, flatness, concentricity. Standard on all tight-tolerance and first-article orders. Available on request for standard tolerance work.

**First Article Inspection Report (FAI)**
Full dimensional layout on the first piece of a new production run. Available in AS9102 (aerospace) or AIAG PPAP (automotive) format on request.

**Process Records**
For surface finishing orders: coating thickness measurements, bath chemistry records, and visual inspection sign-off. Masking requirements documented per drawing.

## Section 10: FAQ

**Design:** Accordion. White background.

**H2:** Precision Machining Services — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerances can TOP PROTO hold on precision CNC machined parts?**
Standard CNC work follows ISO 2768-m — ±0.01 mm on linear dimensions. Precision features hold to ±0.005 mm, confirmed by CMM. Wire EDM reaches ±0.002–0.005 mm on 2D profiles through conductive materials. For CNC turning, diametral tolerances of ±0.005 mm are achievable on bearing seats and valve seats. Achievable tolerance on any specific feature depends on geometry, material, and tool access — the engineering team assesses tolerance feasibility during DFM review and flags any callout at the edge of practical capability before cutting starts.

**Q2: How long does precision CNC machining take at TOP PROTO?**
Standard aluminium CNC prototypes ship in 1–5 business days from the Dongguan facility. Rush CNC orders can be machined in 1–3 business days depending on geometry and material availability. Stainless steel and titanium typically take 3–5 business days. 5-axis parts with complex geometry: 4–7 business days. Every quote includes a confirmed lead time — not an estimate. If your timeline is tight, flag it when uploading your file.

**Q3: What quality documentation comes with every precision machining order?**
Every order includes a dimensional inspection report, material certificate traceable to heat number, and Certificate of Conformance. Tight-tolerance and first-article orders include CMM inspection reports as standard. Surface finishing orders include process records and coating thickness measurements. No documentation fees on standard orders. First Article Inspection in AS9102 or AIAG PPAP format is available on request for production programmes.

**Q4: What is the minimum order quantity for precision CNC machining?**
No minimum. One part is a valid order on CNC milling, turning, 5-axis, and wire EDM. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup time is fixed regardless of quantity — but there is no order floor. A single prototype receives the same material verification, dimensional inspection, and shipping documentation as a 50-piece batch.

**Q5: What file formats does TOP PROTO accept for CNC machining quotes?**
STEP (.step / .stp) is preferred for all 3D geometry — it carries full surface data and is what the engineering team uses for toolpath planning and tolerance confirmation. IGES, DXF, DWG, SolidWorks (.sldprt), CATIA (.CATPart), Parasolid (.x_t), and PDF drawings are also accepted. Attach your 2D drawing PDF alongside the 3D STEP file when tight-tolerance callouts or GD&T requirements appear on the drawing — the drawing becomes the tolerance authority.

**Q6: What materials does TOP PROTO machine for precision work?**
CNC machining metals: aluminium (6061, 7075, 2024, 5052), stainless steel (303, 304, 316L, 17-4PH), titanium (Grade 2, Grade 5), brass (C360), copper (C101, C110), tool steel, steel alloy (4140, 4340), and bronze. Engineering plastics: PEEK, Delrin (POM), nylon (PA6, PA66), polycarbonate, ABS, PTFE, acrylic, HDPE, Ultem, and polypropylene. Confirm your specific grade and condition at quote stage — different grades of the same alloy family have different machinability, tool wear, and tolerance behaviour.

**Q7: How much do precision machining services cost at TOP PROTO?**
CNC machining cost depends on material, geometry complexity, tolerance, quantity, and surface finish. A simple aluminium 6061 bracket with standard ISO 2768-m tolerances costs significantly less than a complex titanium Grade 5 component with ±0.005 mm positional callouts and hard anodizing. Upload your STEP file for pricing within 24 hours — not a range or estimate. DFM review is included in the quote at no additional charge. No separate setup fee appears on the quote.

**Q8: How does TOP PROTO compare to online CNC machining platforms like Xometry or Protolabs?**
Xometry routes orders across a global network of partner shops — quality, lead time, and communication vary by shop. Protolabs offers automated quoting for standard geometries at premium US and EU pricing. TOP PROTO is a direct manufacturer: one facility in Dongguan quotes, machines, inspects, and ships every order. Tolerances to ±0.005 mm, CMM-verified. DFM review included with every quote. Dimensional inspection report, material certifications, and Certificate of Conformance ship with every order as standard — not on request. For engineers outside the US and EU, or for projects combining multiple processes from one facility, TOP PROTO is typically faster and meaningfully less expensive.
