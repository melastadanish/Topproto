## Section 3b: Rapid Tooling vs Production Tooling
**Design:** 2-column comparison or table. White background.
**Heading:** Rapid Tooling vs Production Tooling — Which Do You Need?

| | Rapid Tooling (Aluminium) | Production Tooling (Steel) |
|---|---|---|
| Tool material | Aluminium (7075) | P20 / H13 / S136 hardened steel |
| Lead time | 5–10 days | 3–6 weeks |
| Tool cost | Low | High |
| Tool life | 1,000–10,000 shots | 100,000–1,000,000+ shots |
| Cavity finish | Good — SPI B1/B2 standard | Excellent — SPI A1/A2 achievable |
| Part tolerance | ±0.1mm | ±0.05mm |
| Best for | Prototypes, pre-production, bridge runs | Full production |

## Section 8: The Process

**Design:** RS-02 variant — 6-step timeline.
1. **DFM Review** — Geometry, draft angles, parting lines, gating assessed
2. **Tool Design** — Mould designed in CAD — cavity, core, runners, cooling channels
3. **Tool Machining** — Aluminium or steel block CNC machined to form
4. **First Article** — T1 sample produced, dimensional report issued
5. **Production Run** — Parts moulded to approved T1 standard
6. **Inspection & Delivery** — QC against drawing, packed and shipped
