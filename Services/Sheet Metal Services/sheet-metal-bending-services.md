## CNC Press Brake Bending for Sheet Metal Prototypes

Sheet metal bending at TOP PROTO uses CNC press brake machines to form flat metal blanks into three-dimensional parts at their Dongguan, China facility. Bend angle tolerance is ±1.0°. Minimum bend radius is 1× material thickness — the point below which most aluminium alloys and work-hardened stainless grades begin to crack at the outer bend face. ISO 9001:2015 certified. Springback compensation is programmed per material grade so the part springs to the correct angle, not the overbent angle. Single prototype parts accepted, 3-business-day lead time.

Press brake bending — also called CNC bending — applies a hardened punch to the top face of a flat blank, forcing the sheet down into a matched die. The sheet bends at the contact line, forming the angle determined by how far the punch travels. A k-factor — the ratio of the neutral axis position to material thickness — governs how much flat blank length goes into each bend. Programming the correct k-factor per material grade ensures the flat blank, once bent, produces the correct overall dimensions. Getting the k-factor wrong means every part in the batch comes out at the wrong developed length.

Springback is the elastic return of the sheet after the punch retracts. Stainless steel 304 springs back more than aluminium 5052-H32 at the same bend angle. The CNC program overbends to the calculated compensation angle so the part relaxes to the target angle. Measure the first part, confirm it hits ±1.0°, and the batch runs with confidence.

## CNC Press Brake Bending vs. Roll Forming — Which Process Fits Your Part

| Factor | CNC Press Brake Bending | Roll Forming |
|---|---|---|
| Suitable geometry | Straight bends, multi-bend enclosures, channels, brackets | Continuous curved profiles, long runs of constant cross-section |
| Bend angle control | ±1.0° | ±1.0–2.0° depending on setup |
| Minimum lot size | 1 part — no minimum | Typically 50–100 parts minimum for setup justification |
| Part complexity | Multi-bend sequences with different angles per bend | Single cross-section repeated over part length |
| Setup change time | Low — tool change per bend sequence | High — roll setup requires significant adjustment |
| Lead time (prototype) | 3 business days | Not suited to prototype quantities |
| Tooling cost | No dedicated tooling — standard press brake dies | Significant roll tooling investment |

Press brake bending is the correct process for prototype and low-volume sheet metal parts. Roll forming requires long runs to justify the setup cost and is not appropriate for 1–50 part quantities. If your enclosure has 4 different bend angles across 3 different flange lengths, the press brake programs each bend in sequence — no dedicated tooling required.

## Design Guidelines for CNC Bending

| Feature | Recommendation | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Min bend radius | 1× material thickness (1.5× for 6061-T6) | Tighter radii cause cracking on the outer bend face of aluminium alloys and stainless |
| Min flange length | 3× material thickness | Shorter flanges cannot seat on press brake tooling and produce inconsistent angles |
| Relief cuts at bend corners | Required at inside corners adjacent to bends | Without relief, material tears at the junction between bend lines |
| Hole to bend distance | 2× material thickness minimum | Closer holes distort during bending — oval holes and shifted positions result |
| Thread holes | Tap after bending | Tapping before bending risks thread distortion — specify "tap after bend" on drawing |
| Multiple bend directions | Minimise where possible | Bends in opposite directions add handling operations and increase setup time |
| Tolerances on mating flanges | Call out explicitly in drawing | Default is ±0.2mm — tighter mating flanges need a direct tolerance note |
| K-factor | Specify material grade, not k-factor | TOP PROTO applies the correct k-factor per material grade in the bend program |
| Bend sequence | Shown as numbered notes on drawing if critical | Where bend sequence affects access, number the bends on the drawing |
| Flat blank | DXF of unfolded blank preferred | Folded STEP also accepted — our engineers calculate the correct flat blank with bend allowance |

The DFM review checks all design guidelines before bending starts. Short flanges, tight radii, holes too close to bend lines — these are caught at the quote stage, not discovered on the first formed part.

## Sheet Metal Bending — Frequently Asked Questions

**What bend angle tolerance can TOP PROTO hold on CNC press brake work?**

Bend angle tolerance on CNC press brake bending at TOP PROTO is ±1.0° per bend. This is achieved through springback compensation programmed per material grade — the press brake overbends to a calculated angle, and the sheet relaxes to the target after the punch retracts. First-off parts are measured with a digital protractor before the batch proceeds. If your assembly requires tighter angular control than ±1.0°, discuss it at the DFM stage — some geometries allow tighter holds depending on material thickness and flange configuration.

**What is springback and how does it affect my bent part dimensions?**

Springback is the elastic return of the sheet metal after the press brake punch retracts — the part opens slightly from the formed angle because the metal's elastic zone partially recovers. Stainless steel 304 springs back more than aluminium 5052-H32 at the same target angle, because stainless has a higher elastic modulus relative to its yield strength. TOP PROTO programs springback compensation into the CNC bend sequence per material grade — the machine overbends to the compensation angle, the part springs to the target. Without this compensation, every part would come out at the wrong angle and the error would be consistent across the entire batch.

**What is the minimum bend radius for sheet metal parts?**

Minimum bend radius at TOP PROTO is 1× material thickness for most materials — aluminium 5052-H32, stainless 304 and 316L, mild steel SPCC and Q235, brass, and copper. Aluminium 6061-T6 is less ductile than 5052-H32 and the recommended minimum bend radius is 1.5× material thickness to avoid cracking at the outer face of the bend. Tighter radii than these minimums cause crack initiation in the tensile zone of the outer bend face — the crack typically propagates across the flange width and the part is scrap. The DFM review flags any bend radius below the material minimum before the first part is formed.

**What is the minimum flange length for CNC press brake bending?**

Minimum flange length is 3× material thickness. Shorter flanges cannot seat correctly on the press brake die surface — the flange slides under the die lip and the bend angle becomes unpredictable. For a 2mm thick sheet, the minimum flange is 6mm. For 1mm sheet, the minimum is 3mm. If your design has a flange shorter than this minimum, the DFM review will flag it and suggest either increasing the flange length or adding a bend relief to allow the short flange to form correctly.

**What file formats does TOP PROTO accept for sheet metal bending quotes?**

DXF of the unfolded flat blank is the preferred file format for sheet metal bending. It gives the bend program the exact blank geometry including hole positions, relief cuts, and the correct flat blank developed length. STEP files of the folded 3D part are also accepted — the engineering team calculates the correct flat blank using the k-factor for your specified material grade. PDF drawings alongside the DXF are useful for capturing bend angle callouts, tolerance notes, and any features that require tighter than standard tolerances. Include material grade, sheet thickness, and required quantity in the upload.

**What is bend allowance and do I need to calculate it for my drawing?**

Bend allowance is the amount of flat blank length consumed by a single bend — the arc length along the neutral axis of the bend zone. It depends on bend radius, material thickness, and the k-factor for the specific material grade. You do not need to calculate bend allowance for your DXF. If you supply a folded STEP model or a flat blank DXF, TOP PROTO calculates the correct developed flat blank dimensions using the k-factor for your specified material grade. If you supply a flat blank DXF with the bend lines marked, confirm in the notes whether the blank already accounts for bend allowance or whether you want TOP PROTO to recalculate it.

**What is the minimum order quantity for sheet metal bending?**

There is no minimum order quantity. TOP PROTO accepts single prototype parts — one bracket, one enclosure shell, one panel. This applies to both laser cutting and CNC bending orders. Prototype pricing is per-part and carries higher unit cost than a 50-part batch, but a single-part order is always valid. Once your prototype design is confirmed, low-volume batches typically ship in 1–3 weeks.

**How does CNC press brake bending compare to roll forming for prototype sheet metal work?**

Roll forming produces parts with a constant cross-section by feeding sheet through a series of roller dies that progressively shape the profile. It is efficient for long runs of the same cross-section — channels, angles, and hat sections — but requires dedicated roll tooling that costs significantly more than standard press brake dies. Roll forming is not practical for prototype quantities because the setup cost cannot be amortised over a small batch. CNC press brake bending requires no dedicated tooling for standard bend angles, accepts single parts, and handles multi-bend sequences with different angles and flange lengths on the same part. For prototype and low-volume sheet metal work under 50 parts, press brake bending is the correct process in all but the most unusual cross-section requirements.
