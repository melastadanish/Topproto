## Sheet Metal Fabrication for Prototypes and Low-Volume Parts

Sheet metal fabrication converts flat sheet stock into three-dimensional parts through cutting and forming operations. TOP PROTO runs two core sheet metal processes — laser cutting and CNC press brake bending — at their Dongguan, China facility. The result: brackets, enclosures, panels, and structural frames produced to ±0.2mm general tolerance (ISO 2768-c), with bend angle tolerance of ±1.0°. ISO 9001:2015 certified. Single prototype parts accepted, with a 3-business-day lead time.

Laser cutting produces flat blanks with cut profile tolerance of ±0.1mm. Kerf width runs 0.1–0.3mm depending on material and thickness. Complex profiles, curved edges, slot patterns, and hole arrays cut in a single setup — the blanking operation that defines every downstream bend sequence.

CNC bending shapes flat blanks into finished 3D geometry using a press brake with precision-ground tooling. Bend radius starts at 1× material thickness to avoid crack initiation. The k-factor — the ratio describing where the neutral axis sits during bending — is programmed per material grade to control springback and achieve the specified flat blank dimensions.

Both processes share one DXF source file. Your cut profile and your bend program come from the same flat geometry — so one DFM review covers the full part.

## Sheet Metal Fabrication Processes

### Laser Cutting — Flat Profiles to ±0.1mm

Laser cutting is the first operation for any bent sheet metal part. A focused laser beam traces the flat blank outline, cutting through the sheet material and generating the cut profile, hole pattern, and any internal features. Kerf width — the material removed by the laser beam — runs 0.1–0.3mm and is accounted for in the cut program so finished profile dimensions hit ±0.1mm tolerance.

**Best for:** Complex flat profiles, curved outlines, dense hole patterns, prototype blanks, multi-part nesting from a single sheet.

**Key capabilities:**
- Cut profile tolerance: ±0.1mm
- Kerf width: 0.1–0.3mm
- Min sheet thickness: 0.5mm
- Max sheet thickness (brass, copper): 5mm
- Lead time for blanks: 1–2 business days
- Materials: aluminium, stainless steel, mild steel, brass, copper

**When laser cutting is the right process:** Your part has curves, internal cutouts, or a hole pattern that would require multiple drill setups in a conventional shop. Laser cutting handles all of those in one program pass — nesting multiple different blank profiles on the same sheet to minimize material waste.

### CNC Bending — Press Brake Forming to ±1.0°

CNC press brake bending forms flat blanks into three-dimensional parts. A hardened punch and die set apply controlled force to the blank along a programmed bend line, forming the sheet to the target angle. Bend angle tolerance is ±1.0°. Minimum bend radius is 1× material thickness — tighter radii cause crack initiation on aluminium alloys and work-hardened stainless grades.

**Best for:** Enclosures, brackets, channels, panels, flanged frames — any geometry where a flat blank needs to become 3D.

**Key capabilities:**
- Bend angle tolerance: ±1.0°
- Min bend radius: 1× material thickness
- Min flange length: 3× material thickness
- Lead time: 1–3 days after blank is cut
- Springback compensation programmed per material grade

**When press brake bending is the right process:** Your part has one or more bends and needs consistent angular geometry across a batch. Complex multi-bend sequences — where each bend references the previous — are sequenced in the CNC program to maintain the correct bend allowance at every station.

## Sheet Metal Design Guidelines

| Feature | Recommendation | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Min hole diameter | 1.2× sheet thickness | Smaller diameters tear during laser cutting; secondary drilling required |
| Hole to edge distance | 2× sheet thickness minimum | Less clearance causes material distortion near the cut edge |
| Min flange length | 3× material thickness | Shorter flanges cannot seat correctly on press brake tooling |
| Min bend radius | 1× material thickness | Tighter radii cause cracking on aluminium alloys and stainless |
| Relief cuts at bend corners | Required at inside corners >90° | Without relief, material tears at the corner junction |
| Consistent bend direction | Design bends in the same direction where possible | Opposite-direction bends add handling operations and cost |
| Thread holes | Tap after bending, not before | Tapping a flat blank risks thread distortion during bending |
| Mating flange tolerances | Call out explicitly in drawing | Default sheet metal tolerance is ±0.2mm — tighter features need a tolerance note |
| File format | DXF with unfolded flat blank | Folded STEP also accepted — our engineers unfold it before quoting |

Your DFM review covers all these checks before the first cut. If your design violates a fabrication minimum, you receive a note with the quote — not a scrapped part.

## Sheet Metal Fabrication — Frequently Asked Questions

**What tolerances can sheet metal fabrication achieve at TOP PROTO?**

Sheet metal fabrication at TOP PROTO holds ±0.2mm general tolerance (ISO 2768-c) across flat blank dimensions and hole locations. Laser cut profiles achieve ±0.1mm on the cut outline — tighter than the general sheet metal tolerance because the laser program directly controls cut path. Bend angles hold ±1.0° using CNC press brake forming. If your drawing calls out tighter tolerances on specific features, add a tolerance note directly on the drawing — the DFM review will confirm whether the spec is achievable before fabrication starts.

**What processes does your sheet metal service include?**

TOP PROTO sheet metal fabrication covers laser cutting and CNC press brake bending. Laser cutting produces flat blanks with complex profiles, hole patterns, and internal cutouts. CNC bending forms those blanks into three-dimensional geometry — brackets, enclosures, channels, and panels. Welding, stamping, and punching are not part of the sheet metal service at TOP PROTO. If your design requires welded assemblies, discuss the requirement during the DFM review — the team can advise on alternative geometry that achieves the structural intent without welding.

**What materials can you process for sheet metal fabrication?**

TOP PROTO processes aluminium (5052-H32 and 6061-T6), stainless steel (304 and 316L), mild steel (SPCC and Q235), brass (C27400 and C36000), and copper (C101 and C110). Sheet thickness starts at 0.5mm for all grades. Brass and copper run up to 5mm. Aluminium 5052-H32 has better formability than 6061-T6 — specify your grade in the upload and the DFM review will flag any bend radius concerns for the grade you've chosen.

**What file formats do you accept for sheet metal quoting?**

DXF is the preferred format for flat sheet metal profiles — it gives the cut program the exact geometry without interpretation. STEP files are also accepted for folded 3D models; the engineering team unfolds them before generating the cut and bend programs. Include material grade, sheet thickness, and required quantity in the enquiry. PDF drawings with tolerance callouts are useful alongside the DXF to capture any features that require tighter-than-standard tolerances.

**What are the design minimums I need to meet for sheet metal parts?**

Minimum hole diameter is 1.2× sheet thickness — smaller holes tear during laser cutting and require secondary drilling. Hole-to-edge clearance should be at least 2× sheet thickness to avoid edge distortion. Minimum bend radius is 1× material thickness to prevent cracking on aluminium and stainless grades. Minimum flange length is 3× material thickness — shorter flanges don't seat correctly on press brake tooling. Inside corners at bends greater than 90° require relief cuts. All of these are checked during the DFM review.

**How do you verify that sheet metal parts are within tolerance?**

Every order includes a DFM review before cutting starts. First-off parts are measured against the flat blank drawing — cut profile dimensions, hole positions, and hole diameters — before the batch proceeds. Bend angles are verified on the first formed part with a digital protractor. Dimensional inspection reports are available on request. Material certifications are available on request. Critical features can be measured by CMM where the tolerance requirement justifies it.

**What is the minimum order quantity for sheet metal fabrication?**

There is no minimum order quantity. TOP PROTO accepts single prototype parts — one bracket, one enclosure panel, one frame component. This applies to laser cutting and CNC bending work. Prototype runs are priced per part, so a single-part order carries a higher unit cost than a 50-part batch, but it is always a valid order. Once your design is confirmed on the prototype, low-volume batches typically ship in 1–3 weeks.

**How does TOP PROTO compare to a local sheet metal shop for prototype work?**

A local sheet metal shop typically requires a minimum batch quantity — often 10 to 25 parts — to offset setup cost. TOP PROTO accepts single parts with no batch minimum. Lead time for a prototype from a local shop is often 1–2 weeks; TOP PROTO ships prototype sheet metal parts in 3 business days. DFM feedback from a local shop is informal and phone-based; TOP PROTO delivers a written DFM review with every quote, covering bend radii, hole sizes, edge distances, and flange lengths. The cost difference from Dongguan, China manufacturing typically offsets international shipping on any order above 5 parts.
