## Section 3: What is CNC Milling?

**Design:** Single column. White background.

**Heading:** What is CNC Milling?

CNC milling is a subtractive manufacturing process in which a rotating multi-point cutting tool moves across a workpiece to remove material and produce the specified geometry. The machine follows a toolpath derived from your CAD file — reproducing features precisely, part after part.

TOP PROTO runs 3-axis, 4-axis, and simultaneous 5-axis milling configurations in one facility. We machine aluminium, stainless steel, titanium, brass, copper, tool steel, and engineering plastics including PEEK, polycarbonate, POM, and nylon. Every milling order includes a DFM review before production and a dimensional inspection report before shipment.

## Section 4: Related Processes

**Design:** 4-card grid. Light grey background. Each card: process name + description + CTA link.

**Heading:** Related CNC Processes

**CNC Routing**
High-speed cutting for large-format or lower-density materials — composites, aluminium plate, and engineering plastics. Used for parts requiring large flat areas, profiled edges, and cutouts beyond standard milling envelope dimensions.
→ See CNC Routing

**Wire EDM**
Cuts precise profiles through conductive materials using an electrically charged wire. Applied for features that require sharp internal corners, thin ribs, or geometries in hardened materials that conventional cutting tools cannot access cleanly.
→ See Wire EDM Services

**CNC Milling**
Rotating cutters for pockets, slots, bores, chamfers, and complex contoured surfaces. 3-axis and 5-axis configurations for both simple flat-featured parts and components with multi-sided or curved geometry.
→ See CNC Milling Services

**CNC Turning**
Produces cylindrical, conical, and threaded features by rotating the workpiece against a fixed cutting tool. Shafts, bushings, fittings, and round-profiled components — transferred to milling for secondary features where required.
→ See CNC Turning Services

## Section 11: Surface Finishes

**Design:** 4-card grid. Light grey background.

**Heading:** CNC Milling Surface Finishes

**As Machined**
Retains the surface condition from the cutting tool. Ra 3.2µm standard, tool paths may be visible. Used for internal structural parts, fixtures, and components where surface appearance is not specified.

**Bead Blasting**
Uniform, non-directional matte texture from fine media. Removes tool marks, reduces gloss, produces consistent appearance. Commonly applied before anodizing to improve colour uniformity.

**Anodizing (Type II / Type III)**
Grows a hard aluminium oxide layer through electrolytic oxidation. Type II: 5–25µm, standard corrosion resistance and colour. Type III hard anodizing: 25–150µm, wear-critical surfaces.

**Mirror Polish**
Progressive mechanical buffing to Ra 0.1µm or below. Reflective finish specified for optical housings, decorative panels, and medical instrument components.

**Heading:** Advantages of CNC Milling

**High Dimensional Accuracy**
Milled parts reproduce your CAD geometry feature-for-feature. Standard tolerance ±0.01mm — precision features to ±0.005mm. Every part measured against your drawing before shipment.

**Isotropic Mechanical Strength**
Material properties are consistent in every direction. Milled aluminium, titanium, and stainless steel parts carry the same mechanical spec as the parent billet — no layer interfaces, no anisotropy.

**Material Efficiency**
Precision toolpaths minimise waste. Off-cuts from prototype runs can often be repurposed for secondary test pieces or smaller companion parts.

**Complex Geometry in One Setup**
5-axis simultaneous milling produces compound angles, undercuts, and multi-sided features without repositioning. No datum shift between setups — critical for tight positional tolerances.

## Section 17: FAQ

**Heading:** CNC Milling Services — Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What materials can TOP PROTO CNC mill?**
We mill a broad range of metals and engineering plastics. Metals include aluminium 6061 and 7075, stainless steel 304 and 316L, titanium Grade 5, brass C360, copper C101, and tool steels A2, D2, and H13. Engineering plastics include PEEK, polycarbonate, POM (Delrin), nylon PA6 and PA66, ABS, PTFE, PMMA, HDPE, and Ultem. Material is verified against certification before production begins.

**2. What tolerance can you hold on CNC milled parts?**
Standard tolerance is ±0.01mm for metals (ISO 2768-F) and plastics (ISO 2768-M). Precision features are held to ±0.005mm where your drawing requires it. 5-axis simultaneous milling achieves ±0.003mm on critical compound-angle features. All tolerances are confirmed at quoting and verified by dimensional inspection before shipment.

**3. How quickly can I get CNC milled parts?**
Standard prototype lead time for aluminium is 3–5 business days. Stainless steel and titanium typically take 5–7 business days depending on geometry complexity and number of setups. Expedited options are available for time-critical orders — confirm at the time of quoting.

**4. Can I order both prototypes and low-volume production runs?**
Yes. TOP PROTO handles orders from a single prototype part through to low-volume production batches of 500 parts. Prototype parts run on the same machines, to the same tolerances and inspection standard, as your production order. No supplier change needed when you move from development to production.

**5. How does your quality control process work for CNC milling?**
Parts go through three inspection stages: incoming material verification against certification, in-process dimensional checks at defined machining intervals, and final inspection against your drawing before shipment approval. Every order ships with a dimensional inspection report, material certifications traceable to heat number, and a Certificate of Conformance. First Article Inspection Reports are available for production programmes.

**6. What file formats do you accept for CNC milling quotes?**
STEP files are preferred — they retain full 3D geometry and all feature references. We also accept IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), CATIA (.CATPart), Parasolid (.x_t), DXF, and DWG. PDF engineering drawings are accepted alongside CAD files and are treated as the authoritative tolerance and GD&T source.

**7. What is the minimum order quantity for CNC milling?**
No minimum order quantity. TOP PROTO accepts single-part prototype orders. A 1-part order receives the same material verification, DFM review, and dimensional inspection report as a 100-part batch.

**8. How does TOP PROTO compare to other CNC milling suppliers?**
Unlike marketplace platforms that route orders to third-party shops, TOP PROTO is a direct manufacturer — one facility, one team, accountable quality on every order. We include DFM review with every quote at no additional cost and ship dimensional inspection reports as standard, not on request. Our Dongguan facility provides direct-manufacturer pricing without broker markup.
