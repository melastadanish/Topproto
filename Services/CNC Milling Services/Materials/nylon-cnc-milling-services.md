## Section 2: Why Nylon for CNC Milling

**H2:** Why Nylon Is Specified for High-Strength Plastic Milled Components

Nylon (polyamide, PA) is specified for CNC milled parts where ABS and Delrin are insufficient in strength, toughness, or load capacity. PA6 achieves 75 MPa tensile strength — 50% higher than ABS (40–50 MPa) and 15% higher than Delrin POM-H (65 MPa). PA6-GF30 (30% glass-filled) reaches 130 MPa, approaching aluminium territory at one-quarter the density. For structural plastic components subject to sustained load — brackets, gears under load, bearing housings — nylon's higher strength-to-weight ratio extends the applicability of plastic machining into applications that would otherwise require aluminium.

Nylon's self-lubricating property is more pronounced than Delrin's in high-load applications. Nylon's amide groups absorb microscopic amounts of moisture that act as internal plasticisers, reducing stick-slip friction in bearing surfaces. Nylon-on-steel CoF in wet or humid conditions is 0.10–0.20 — competitive with or better than Delrin in high-load bearing applications. Nylon is the standard material for plastic bearing bushings, wear plates, and sliding guides where load is higher than Delrin can sustain.

The critical design consideration for precision nylon milling is moisture absorption. PA6 absorbs 2.5–3.5% moisture at equilibrium in normal ambient conditions. Each percent of moisture absorption causes 0.2–0.3% linear dimensional change — a 100 mm PA6 part can change dimensions by 0.5–1.0 mm between bone-dry and fully saturated. For precision milled nylon parts (gears, precision fits, tight-tolerance assemblies), the engineering team designs to the moisture equilibrium dimension — the part is either conditioned before final measurement or machined with moisture expansion allowance built into the target dimensions. Confirm the moisture management strategy at DFM stage for any precision nylon application.

PA66 (nylon 66) provides better thermal performance and slightly lower moisture uptake than PA6 — HDT at 250°C (dry) vs PA6's 220°C, and equilibrium moisture absorption of 2.0–2.5% vs 2.5–3.5% for PA6. PA66 is specified when the part operates at elevated temperature or when slightly better dimensional stability is required. PA66-GF30 (90% glass-filled by stiffness contribution) achieves 160 MPa tensile — the highest strength of standard milled polyamide grades.

## Section 3: Nylon Milling — Material Properties and Machinability

**H2:** Nylon Grades for CNC Milling

| Grade | Tensile Strength | HDT (1.8 MPa) | Moisture (equilib.) | Key Property | Best Application |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PA6 | 75 MPa | 63°C (wet) / 220°C (dry) | 2.5–3.5% | Impact resistance, self-lubrication | Bearings, gears, structural brackets |
| PA66 | 85 MPa | 66°C (wet) / 250°C (dry) | 2.0–2.5% | Higher thermal, lower moisture | Automotive, elevated temperature |
| PA6-GF30 | 130 MPa | 200°C | <1.0% | High stiffness, low CTE | Precision structural, load-bearing |
| PA66-GF30 | 160 MPa | 250°C | <1.0% | Highest strength PA grade | High-load, high-temperature structural |
| PA6-MoS2 | 75 MPa | 60°C (wet) | 2.5% | Better self-lubrication | Dry-running bearings, slides |

**Machinability:** Nylon machines well with sharp HSS or carbide tooling. Nylon's toughness means it tends to deflect slightly before cutting — sharp tools and positive rake angles are required to shear rather than push the material. PA6 tends to produce stringy chips; compressed air or light mist coolant assists chip clearance. GF30 grades are significantly more abrasive — glass fibres accelerate tool wear; carbide tooling required. Pre-drying nylon stock before machining for precision applications eliminates moisture-related dimensional variation from the machining operation.

## Section 5: Nylon CNC Milling Applications

**H2:** Nylon CNC Milled Parts — Applications and Industries

**Structural Plastic Brackets and Frames** — Load-bearing brackets, structural frames, and support components where ABS is insufficiently strong. PA6's 75 MPa tensile and excellent impact resistance (notched Izod 4–10 kJ/m² depending on grade) suit structural applications at moderate weight. GF30 grades extend nylon into structural applications at 130–160 MPa.

**Bearings and Wear Plates** — Plain bearings, bushing inserts, and wear plate liners for conveyor systems, guides, and sliding mechanisms. Nylon's self-lubrication and load capacity under wet or humid conditions exceed Delrin's in high-load bearing applications. PA6-MoS2 provides enhanced self-lubrication for dry-running bearing surfaces.

**Gears and Drive Components** — Milled nylon gears, worm wheel blanks, and drive sprockets for light-to-medium power transmission. Nylon's impact resistance protects against shock loading that would fracture acetal or acrylic gears. Nylon gears running on steel worms are the standard specification for low-cost, quiet power transmission in office automation and consumer machinery.

**Automotive Engine Bay Components** — Thermally stable structural brackets, cable guides, and covers for underhood automotive applications. PA66 and PA66-GF30 are specified for engine bay temperatures above 100°C where standard PA6 would approach its wet HDT limit. Automotive under-hood applications use PA66-GF30 extensively for intake manifolds, thermostat housings, and cable management.

**Industrial Equipment Wear Parts** — Chain guides, star wheels, pusher pads, and conveyor components for material handling and packaging machinery. Nylon's wear resistance, impact resistance, and self-lubrication extend the service life of wear parts in high-cycle machinery compared to ABS or polycarbonate.

## Section 6: FAQ

**H2:** Nylon CNC Milling — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerances are achievable when CNC milling nylon?**
Standard nylon milling tolerance is ±0.1 mm. The primary limitation on tighter tolerances is moisture absorption — PA6 absorbs 2.5–3.5% moisture at equilibrium, and each percent absorption causes 0.2–0.3% linear dimensional change. A 100 mm PA6 part can change dimensions by 0.5–1.0 mm between bone-dry and fully saturated. For precision applications, the engineering team pre-dries nylon stock before machining and conditions parts at the target humidity before final inspection. This process allows ±0.05 mm to be achieved on controlled, moisture-equilibrated nylon parts. Confirm the moisture management strategy at DFM stage for any precision nylon application.

**Q2: What nylon grades do you mill?**
PA6 (nylon 6) is the most commonly milled grade — 75 MPa tensile strength, good impact resistance, excellent self-lubrication, and wide grade availability including natural (off-white) and black. PA66 (nylon 66) provides higher HDT and slightly lower moisture absorption — specified for elevated temperature and better dimensional stability. PA6-GF30 (30% glass-filled) reaches 130 MPa with significantly improved stiffness and lower CTE — for structural and load-bearing applications. PA66-GF30 achieves 160 MPa — the highest strength milled polyamide grade. PA6-MoS2 (molybdenum disulphide filled) provides enhanced dry-running lubrication for bearing applications. Confirm grade availability at quote stage.

**Q3: How does moisture absorption affect CNC milled nylon parts?**
PA6 absorbs 2.5–3.5% moisture at equilibrium in normal indoor conditions (50% relative humidity). This moisture absorption causes linear dimensional change of 0.2–0.3% per percent absorbed — meaning a fully dry PA6 part machined to size will grow by 0.5–1.0 mm on a 100 mm dimension when it equilibrates to ambient humidity. For precision gears and tight-fit components, three approaches manage this: machine to moisture-equilibrated dimensions (allow part to absorb moisture to equilibrium before final inspection); machine with a humidity allowance (machine slightly undersized to account for growth); or specify a glass-filled grade (PA6-GF30 absorbs <1.0% moisture and has significantly better dimensional stability). The engineering team confirms the moisture management strategy at DFM stage.

**Q4: What surface finishes are available for CNC milled nylon parts?**
Nylon's low surface energy makes conventional coating adhesion unreliable without adhesion promoter or plasma pre-treatment. For most nylon applications — bearings, gears, structural components — the as-milled surface (Ra 1.6 µm, improvable to Ra 0.8 µm with fine finish) is the correct specification. Painting is possible with adhesion promoter — confirm at quote stage. Anodizing, passivation, electropolishing, and black oxide are metal-specific processes not applicable to nylon. For decorative or coloured nylon, specify a coloured grade in the raw material (natural, black, and some custom colours are available) rather than painting.

**Q5: What is the minimum order quantity for CNC milled nylon parts?**
No minimum. CNC milling is applied to any nylon order including single prototypes. One part is a valid order.

**Q6: What file formats do you accept for nylon CNC milling?**
STEP (.stp / .step) is the preferred format. Also accepted: IGES, DXF, DWG, STL, PDF, SolidWorks (.sldprt), CATIA (.CATPart), Parasolid (.x_t). For gear profiles and precision bearing dimensions, provide a 2D drawing with explicit tolerance callouts and a note specifying whether dimensions are dry, conditioned, or equilibrated — the engineering team will confirm the inspection protocol.

**Q7: How does nylon milling cost compare to Delrin milling?**
Nylon and Delrin CNC milling costs are comparable at the machining level — both machine at similar speeds with similar tool life. PA6 raw material is generally similar in cost to Delrin per kilogram. GF30 grades cost more than unfilled grades due to material cost and higher tool wear (glass fibres abrade carbide tooling faster than unfilled plastics). The total cost for precision nylon milling may be slightly higher than Delrin when pre-drying and moisture equilibration conditioning are part of the process. Upload your STEP file for exact pricing within 24 hours.

**Q8: When should I choose nylon over Delrin for CNC milled parts?**
Choose nylon when: higher tensile strength is required (PA6 75 MPa vs Delrin 65 MPa; PA6-GF30 130 MPa), the application involves impact loading (nylon is tougher, Delrin is stiffer), the part must resist alkaline chemicals (nylon has better alkali resistance), or glass-filled grades for high structural stiffness are the specification. Choose Delrin when: dimensional stability across humidity is critical (Delrin absorbs <0.2% moisture vs nylon 2.5–3.5%), precision tolerances must be maintained without moisture management, the application is a precision gear or tight-tolerance fit, or the part will be in contact with neutral or mildly acidic fluids. For structural load-bearing plastic components with impact loading, nylon is correct. For precision dimensional applications in variable humidity, Delrin is correct.
