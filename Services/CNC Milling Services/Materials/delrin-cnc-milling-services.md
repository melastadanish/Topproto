## Section 2: Why Delrin for CNC Milling

**H2:** Why Delrin Is the Precision Engineering Plastic for Milling

Delrin (POM-H — polyoxymethylene homopolymer) is the reference precision engineering plastic for CNC milling. Its combination of low moisture absorption (<0.2%), high crystallinity producing dimensional stability, low coefficient of friction (0.10–0.35 vs steel, dry), and stiffness (flexural modulus 2.8–3.1 GPa) addresses the three failure modes that eliminate other plastics from precision milled applications: moisture-driven dimensional change, creep under load, and friction-limited service life in bearing and sliding applications.

Moisture absorption is the primary reason Delrin outperforms nylon in precision milled parts. Nylon (PA6) absorbs 2.5–3.5% moisture at equilibrium — each percent of moisture absorption causes 0.2–0.3% linear dimensional change. A 100 mm nylon part can grow 0.5–1.0 mm between bone-dry and fully wet. Delrin absorbs less than 0.2% moisture — dimensional change from humidity is negligible. For precision gears, cams, and mechanical components that must maintain tight clearances in varying humidity environments, Delrin is the correct specification and nylon is not.

Delrin machines with excellent surface finish — the crystalline structure shears cleanly, producing low surface roughness (Ra 0.8 µm achievable with a fine finish pass) and well-defined edges. Internal corners are achievable to the radius of the end mill. Delrin does not gum or melt at the cutter as ABS can at elevated temperatures — it cuts cleanly with high spindle speeds and sharp carbide or HSS tooling. No coolant is required for most milling operations; compressed air prevents chip re-welding and keeps the cut clean.

Delrin POM-H is distinct from POM copolymer (POM-C, often sold as Acetal). POM-H (Delrin, DuPont trademark) provides higher mechanical stiffness and hardness. POM-C provides better chemical resistance (particularly to alkaline environments) and lower susceptibility to centreline porosity in thick sections. For most precision milled parts, POM-H (Delrin) is the preferred specification. POM-C is specified when alkaline chemical resistance or large cross-section stock (where centreline voids are a concern) is required.

## Section 5: Delrin CNC Milling Applications

**H2:** Delrin CNC Milled Parts — Applications and Industries

**Precision Gears and Cam Mechanisms** — Milled Delrin gears, spur gears, worm gears, and cam profiles. Delrin's dimensional stability (low moisture absorption) ensures gear tooth profiles hold their dimensions across humidity cycles. Self-lubrication eliminates the need for external lubricant in light-duty gear applications. Delrin gear-to-Delrin gear mesh runs dry — suitable for clean-room and food-contact environments.

**Bearings, Bushings, and Sliding Components** — Plain bearings, linear slides, and bushing components. Delrin's low CoF (0.10–0.35 vs steel, dry) and wear resistance make it the standard plastic for dry-running bearing surfaces. Delrin bushings replace bronze bushings in applications where weight reduction and corrosion resistance are required and load is moderate.

**Food and Beverage Processing Components** — Conveyor guides, chain guides, auger flights, and valve components for food and beverage processing. FDA-grade Delrin (21 CFR 177.2470) is cleared for food contact. Delrin's smooth, low-friction surface cleans easily, and the self-lubricating property eliminates lubricant contamination of food products.

**Medical Device Components** — Non-implant, non-fluid-contact medical device components. Delrin's dimensional stability and cleanability make it a standard material for instrument handles, jig components, and enclosure parts. Confirm FDA and biocompatibility requirements at quote stage — standard Delrin is not cleared for implant or prolonged fluid contact.

**Valve Bodies and Fluid Control** — Plastic valve bodies, manifold inserts, and fluid control components for chemical processing. Delrin's chemical resistance to neutral and mildly acidic fluids (but not strong alkalis, concentrated acids, or oxidising environments) suits pharmaceutical and light chemical processing applications at moderate temperature.

## Section 6: FAQ

**H2:** Delrin CNC Milling — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerances are achievable when CNC milling Delrin?**
Standard Delrin milling tolerance is ±0.05 mm. Precision features with stable fixturing and appropriate tooling achieve ±0.025 mm — significantly tighter than is reliably achievable on nylon due to nylon's moisture-driven dimensional change. Delrin's moisture absorption (<0.2%) means machined dimensions are stable across normal humidity ranges. For mating precision plastic components, Delrin's dimensional stability is the reason it is specified over nylon, ABS, or polycarbonate in applications where clearance must be maintained.

**Q2: What is the difference between Delrin (POM-H) and Acetal (POM-C) for CNC milling?**
Delrin refers specifically to DuPont's POM-H (polyoxymethylene homopolymer). POM-C (polyoxymethylene copolymer, commonly sold as Acetal) differs in molecular structure: POM-H has a homopolymer backbone providing higher mechanical stiffness and hardness; POM-C has a copolymer backbone providing better chemical resistance (particularly to strong alkalis and oxidising environments) and lower susceptibility to centreline porosity in thick cross-section stock. For most precision milled gear, bearing, and mechanical components, POM-H (Delrin) is specified for its higher stiffness. POM-C is specified for chemical handling components and large cross-section parts where porosity is a concern.

**Q3: Is Delrin suitable for food contact applications?**
Yes — FDA-compliant Delrin is available, cleared under 21 CFR 177.2470 for food contact. Delrin is used in food processing conveyor guides, chain wear strips, valve components, and auger flights. Delrin's self-lubricating surface eliminates lubricant contamination in food contact applications, and the smooth milled surface cleans effectively for food hygiene requirements. Confirm FDA grade at quote stage — standard and FDA-grade Delrin are both available; the FDA grade must be specified on the order.

**Q4: What surface finishes are available for CNC milled Delrin parts?**
As-milled Delrin is the standard finish — Ra 1.6 µm with standard pass, Ra 0.8 µm with fine finishing pass. Delrin's low surface energy makes coating adhesion difficult: paint, plating, and most adhesives do not adhere reliably to as-milled Delrin without specialist adhesion promoter or plasma treatment. For most Delrin applications — gears, bearings, guides, valve components — the as-milled finish is the correct specification. Anodizing and passivation are metal-specific processes not applicable to Delrin.

**Q5: What is the minimum order quantity for CNC milled Delrin parts?**
No minimum. CNC milling is applied to any Delrin order including single prototypes. One part is a valid order.

**Q6: What file formats do you accept for Delrin CNC milling?**
STEP (.stp / .step) is the preferred format. Also accepted: IGES, DXF, DWG, STL, PDF, SolidWorks (.sldprt), CATIA (.CATPart), Parasolid (.x_t). For gear profiles, cams, and precision mechanical features, provide a 2D drawing with explicit tolerance callouts — gear tooth profile tolerance and clearance specifications should be explicitly stated rather than relying on default ISO 2768-m.

**Q7: How does Delrin milling cost compare to nylon milling?**
Delrin and nylon milling costs are similar at the material and machining level — both machine easily, with low tool wear and fast cycle times. Delrin raw material costs slightly more per kilogram than standard PA6 nylon. The total cost for a Delrin prototype is typically comparable to nylon. The specification decision between Delrin and nylon should be made on material properties (Delrin for dimensional stability, nylon for self-lubrication with higher load capacity), not cost — the material cost difference is small relative to the total prototype cost. Upload your STEP file for exact pricing within 24 hours.

**Q8: When should I choose Delrin milling over nylon milling?**
Choose Delrin when dimensional stability is the primary requirement — Delrin absorbs <0.2% moisture vs nylon PA6 at 2.5–3.5%, and each percent moisture absorption in nylon causes 0.2–0.3% linear dimensional change. A precision nylon gear can change dimensions significantly between dry and humid conditions; a Delrin gear is essentially unaffected. Choose Delrin for precision gears, tight-tolerance fits, and applications in varying humidity environments. Choose nylon when: higher tensile strength is required (PA6 75 MPa vs Delrin 65 MPa), the application involves impact loading (nylon is tougher), the part must resist alkaline chemicals (nylon has better alkali resistance than Delrin), or glass-filled grades for high stiffness are specified (PA6-GF30 is widely available). For precision mechanical components, Delrin is the standard. For high-strength, impact-resistant, and high-load plastic parts, nylon is preferred.
