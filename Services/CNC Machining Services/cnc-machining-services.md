## Section 7: Surface Finishes

**Design:** Card grid — 8 finish options. White background.

**Heading:** CNC Machining Surface Finishes

**Subheading:** Functional and aesthetic finishes applied in-house — anodizing, powder coating, passivation, plating, and more.

**As Machined**
Visible tool marks. Ra 3.2µm standard. Most economical option — used where surface appearance is not a requirement.

**Bead Blasting**
Uniform matte or satin texture. Removes tool marks and gives a clean, consistent appearance. Ra 3.2–6.3µm.

**Brushed Finish**
Unidirectional grain from grit polishing. Ra 0.8–1.6µm. Standard for decorative panels and instrument-grade aluminium parts.

**Anodizing (Type II)**
5–25µm oxide layer on aluminium. Corrosion resistance and colour options including clear, black, blue, red, and gold.

**Hard Anodizing (Type III)**
25–150µm hard oxide layer. Wear resistance for aerospace and industrial applications. MIL-A-8625 Type III.

**Black Oxide**
Chemical conversion on steel and stainless steel. Mild corrosion resistance. <1µm — no dimensional change.

**Electropolishing**
Electrochemical material removal on stainless steel. Improves Ra by ~50%. Used on medical and food-grade components.

**Mirror Polish**
Highest polish level. Reflective surface for optical and decorative applications. Ra 0.4–0.8µm achievable.

## Section 13: FAQ

**Heading:** CNC Machining Services — Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What is CNC machining?**
CNC machining is a subtractive manufacturing process where computer-controlled cutting tools remove material from a solid workpiece to produce a finished part. The machine follows a toolpath derived from your CAD file — typically a STEP file — allowing precise, repeatable reproduction of complex geometries. It is the most common method for producing tight-tolerance metal and plastic components for engineering applications.

**2. What tolerance can TOP PROTO hold on CNC machined parts?**
Standard CNC machining tolerance is ±0.01mm (ISO 2768-m). Where your drawing requires tighter features, we achieve ±0.005mm on precision work and ±0.003mm on 5-axis critical features. Tolerances are confirmed at quoting — not assumed. Every part is measured against your specified tolerances before shipment, and a dimensional inspection report is included with every order.

**3. How does TOP PROTO help with CNC prototyping?**
Prototype parts run on the same machines, in the same materials, to the same tolerances as your production design. We review your CAD file for manufacturability before quoting — catching wall thickness, radii, undercut access, and tolerance feasibility issues before cutting starts. You receive a dimensional inspection report with every prototype order, so your test data reflects production-representative parts.

**4. What industries do you serve?**
TOP PROTO machines parts for aerospace (AS9100D), automotive (IATF 16949:2016), medical devices (ISO 13485), robotics, electronics, semiconductor, communication, new energy, and consumer product applications. Each sector has specific tolerance, certification, and documentation requirements — our team applies the right process controls for your application before production begins.

**5. What file formats do you accept?**
STEP files are preferred for CNC machining — they retain full 3D geometry and all feature references. We also accept IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), CATIA (.CATPart), Parasolid (.x_t), STL, DXF, and DWG. PDF engineering drawings are accepted alongside CAD files and are treated as the authoritative tolerance source where dimensions are called out.

**6. What is your minimum order quantity for CNC machining?**
No minimum. TOP PROTO accepts single-part prototype orders through to low-volume batches of 500 parts. Order quantity does not change the material standard, inspection method, or documentation — a single prototype ships with the same dimensional inspection report as a 100-part batch.

**7. How much does CNC machining cost?**
CNC machining cost depends on geometry complexity, material, tolerance requirements, finish, and quantity. Upload your STEP file for an instant price on standard geometries — or within 24 hours for complex parts. DFM feedback is included in the quote at no additional cost.

**8. How does TOP PROTO compare to Xometry or Protolabs?**
Xometry is a marketplace that routes orders to third-party shops — quality and lead time depend on which shop picks up your job. TOP PROTO is a direct manufacturer: one facility, one team, one accountable quality system. Protolabs focuses on US and European customers at premium pricing. TOP PROTO machines from Dongguan at competitive direct-manufacturer pricing, with the same tolerance capability (±0.005mm) and a full DFM review on every quote.
