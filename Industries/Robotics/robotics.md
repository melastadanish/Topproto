## Section 3: Robotics Manufacturing at TOP PROTO

**Heading:** Precision Manufacturing for Robotics

Robotics manufacturing demands tight bearing fits, concentricity on rotating shafts, and lightweight structures that survive repeated dynamic loads. TOP PROTO machines aluminium 6061/7075, titanium Grade 5, PEEK, and stainless steel 316L for robotics OEMs and R&D teams — to tolerances of ±0.005 mm on CNC milled features and ±0.002–0.005 mm on Wire EDM-cut profiles. Every quote includes a DFM review, so fit and tolerance issues surface before a tool is touched.

Joint housings carry the tightest requirements on a robot arm. Bearing bores must hold H7 tolerance — a standard fit specification that, on a 20 mm bore, means a tolerance band of roughly +0.021/0.000 mm — and any deviation shifts the load path through the joint. TOP PROTO machines these features in a single 5-axis setup, eliminating the repositioning error that accumulates when a part transfers between operations.

Concentricity on turned shafts matters equally. Actuator output shafts running in plain or rolling-element bearings need runout — the total deviation of a rotating surface from its true axis — held below 0.01 mm. CNC turning at the Dongguan facility achieves this on steel and aluminium shafts from prototype quantities of one part upward.

Lightweight structural links — topology-optimised arms and brackets — often carry complex internal pockets and thin walls that a 3-axis machine cannot reach cleanly. Five-axis simultaneous machining cuts compound angles and undercuts in a single clamping, preserving the wall thickness your FEA model assumed. ISO 9001:2015 certification governs inspection and documentation at every stage.

## Section 8: Quality & Process

**Heading:** How Quality is Controlled on Robotics Parts

Every robotics order follows a documented inspection sequence. First-article inspection checks every critical dimension against your 2D drawing using CMM — coordinate measuring machine — equipment that measures part geometry to micron accuracy. The report lists every called-out dimension alongside its actual measured value.

Bearing bores and shaft diameters receive dedicated roundness and runout checks. Roundness — how close a circular cross-section is to a perfect circle — is measured at multiple axial positions on each bore. Runout is confirmed on each turned shaft before it leaves the turning centre.

Surface finish (Ra — roughness average, the mean deviation of the surface from a centre line) is confirmed on mating surfaces. As-machined aluminium exits at Ra 3.2 µm. Hard-anodised wear surfaces reach Ra 0.8 µm. Both values are verified by profilometer, not estimated visually.

Dimensional inspection reports are available on request — specify at quoting stage. Material certifications are available on request — specify at quoting stage.

**2. Five-axis in one setup.**  
Complex joint housings with compound angles and intersecting bores are machined in a single clamping. No part transfer means no accumulated positional error between features — your GD&T callouts survive the process.

**3. Wire EDM for gear and cam profiles.**  
Wire EDM — cutting by electrical discharge through a dielectric fluid, with zero mechanical cutting force — holds ±0.002–0.005 mm on intricate profiles. Thin walls are not distorted by tool pressure.

**4. Full material breadth from aluminium to PEEK.**  
Aluminium 6061/7075, titanium Grade 5, stainless steel 316L, and engineering plastics (PEEK, Delrin) are all machined in-house. No material switch requires a new supplier.

**5. DFM review with every quote.**  
The DFM — design for manufacture — review flags potential fit issues: interference at an assembly interface, a wall too thin for the specified tolerance, a feature that requires a process change. It arrives with the price, not as a separate consultation.
