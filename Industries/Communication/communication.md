## Section 3: Communication Manufacturing at TOP PROTO

**Heading:** Precision Manufacturing for Communication & Telecom Parts

TOP PROTO machines communication and telecom components at its Dongguan, China facility — RF cavity filters, waveguide sections, antenna mounts, and base station enclosures delivered to tolerances as tight as ±0.005mm. ISO 9001:2015 certified across every process. DFM review is included with every quote.

RF engineering places demands on manufacturing that general machining shops cannot reliably meet. Surface finish — Ra (roughness average, a measure of surface texture) — directly affects signal insertion loss inside resonant cavities at microwave and millimetre-wave frequencies. TOP PROTO holds Ra 0.8µm or better on cavity internal walls using precision CNC tool paths, which keeps insertion loss predictable across the frequency band.

Dimensional accuracy in resonant cavities is not cosmetic. A resonant cavity — a hollow metallic enclosure tuned to pass or reject specific frequencies — shifts its centre frequency when wall dimensions drift. Cavity wall tolerances held to ±0.01mm or tighter keep filter tuning within design specification without post-machining adjustment. Wire EDM — wire electrical discharge machining, which cuts metal using a controlled electrical spark with no cutting force — holds ±0.002–0.005mm on precision apertures and coupling slots where tool pressure would introduce deflection error.

Outdoor telecom infrastructure components — enclosures, antenna brackets, waveguide flanges — require materials and finishes that withstand UV exposure, moisture ingress, and thermal cycling. TOP PROTO machines stainless steel 304 and aluminium 6061 for these applications and applies anodizing, passivation, or powder coat on request. Prototype batches from a single part. No minimum order quantity.

## Section 11: Frequently Asked Questions

**Heading:** Communication & Telecom Manufacturing — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What certifications does TOP PROTO hold for communication and telecom parts?**
TOP PROTO is ISO 9001:2015 certified — the internationally recognised quality management system requiring documented process control, material traceability, and corrective action procedures. Telecom component manufacturing does not require a single mandatory manufacturing certification, but ISO 9001:2015 is the baseline quality standard most telecom OEMs require from machined parts suppliers. Inspection reports and material certifications are available on request — specify at quoting stage.

**Q2: What tolerance can TOP PROTO hold on RF cavity filters and waveguide components?**
CNC milling holds cavity wall dimensions to ±0.01mm on standard work and to ±0.005mm on critical features with 5-axis machining. Wire EDM — which cuts with an electrical spark and zero cutting force — holds coupling aperture widths to ±0.002–0.005mm, making it the correct process for filter slots where tool pressure causes wall deflection. Surface finish inside cavities reaches Ra 0.8µm or better. Confirm feature-specific tolerances on your 2D drawing when requesting a quote.

**Q3: What materials do you machine for RF and telecom components?**
Aluminium 6061 is the standard for RF cavity housings and waveguide bodies — electrically conductive, light, and machinable to tight tolerances. Copper C101 covers connectors and high-conductivity interfaces. PTFE — polytetrafluoroethylene, a low-loss dielectric polymer — is machined for RF substrate blanks and connector insulators. Stainless steel 304 covers outdoor enclosures and weatherproof hardware. Titanium Grade 5 is available for weight-constrained antenna mounts on satellite or UAV platforms.

**Q4: Do you provide inspection reports and material certifications for telecom parts?**
Inspection reports and material certifications are available on request — specify the requirement at quoting stage so documentation is built into the production plan. Dimensional inspection uses calibrated gauging and CMM for critical RF features. Surface roughness measurement confirms Ra on cavity internal surfaces against the drawing specification. Nothing ships without passing the inspection criteria on the order.

**Q5: What communication and telecom parts do you manufacture most frequently?**
RF cavity filter housings in aluminium 6061 are the most common machined component. Waveguide sections, coupling flanges, and precision transition pieces follow. Antenna mounts and brackets — both machined aluminium and fabricated stainless sheet metal — cover base station and infrastructure applications. PCB shields, EMI gasket frames, and outdoor enclosure panels are produced in sheet metal. Connector bodies are machined from copper or PTFE for low-volume prototyping, and from injection-molded engineering polymers for higher volumes.

**Q6: How long does it take to machine RF cavity and waveguide components?**
CNC machined cavity housings ship in 5 days on standard lead time, or 1–3 days on rush. Wire EDM components — precision apertures and filter slots — ship in 1–5 days. **Q7: How much does machining RF cavity filters cost?**
Cost depends on material, wall thickness, internal feature access, surface finish requirement, and quantity. Aluminium 6061 cavities machine faster and cost less than copper components of equal size. Wire EDM adds cost for precision apertures but eliminates post-machining tuning that looser tolerances would require. Single-part prototype quotes are accepted with no setup penalty beyond standard tooling. Upload your STEP file and 2D drawing to receive a specific price within 24 hours — not a range estimate.

**Q8: How does TOP PROTO compare to a local RF machining shop for cavity filters?**
A local general machining shop often treats RF cavities as standard pockets — surface finish on internal walls is not measured against an Ra specification. TOP PROTO specifies Ra 0.8µm or better as a production requirement for RF cavities, measures it on a surface roughness tester, and reports it. Wire EDM for coupling apertures requires specialist equipment not available at general shops. TOP PROTO runs Wire EDM in-house to ±0.002–0.005mm.
