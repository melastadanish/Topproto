## Section 3: Consumer Products Manufacturing at TOP PROTO

**Heading:** Precision Manufacturing for Consumer Product Development

TOP PROTO produces consumer product components at its Dongguan, China facility — machined aluminium enclosures, vacuum cast pre-production runs, injection molded housings, and appearance-finished parts for product developers moving from concept to validated design. ISO 9001:2015 certified. DFM review is included with every quote.

Consumer product development compresses the time between first prototype and production commitment. The materials, processes, and surface finishes used in prototyping must match the production specification closely enough that fit-and-function validation on the prototype translates directly to the tooled part. TOP PROTO covers the full development path — SLA 3D prints for form study, machined aluminium for functional prototypes, vacuum casting for pre-production runs of 10–50 units, and injection molding when volume justifies tooling investment.

Appearance is a specification, not a preference. Anodizing — an electrochemical process that converts the aluminium surface to a hard oxide layer, providing colour and corrosion resistance — on a machined aluminium enclosure requires Ra 0.8µm or better pre-treatment to achieve a consistent, mark-free result. Powder coat on sheet metal chassis requires clean, degreased, blast-prepped substrate. TOP PROTO controls surface preparation and finishing under the same quality system as machining — the finish specification on your drawing is treated as a dimensional tolerance, not a guideline.

DFM — design for manufacturability — review at quoting stage catches geometry that will create problems at tooling or production. Draft angle — the slight taper on vertical walls that allows a part to release from an injection mold without drag marks or sticking — is flagged on every injection molding quote where it is missing. Sink marks, wall thickness variation, and gate location are identified before T1 tooling is cut, not discovered in the first sample.

## Section 8: Quality & Process (RS-01)

**Heading:** How TOP PROTO Controls Quality for Consumer Product Parts

Appearance is validated before anodizing — not after. Surface roughness on aluminium enclosures is measured to Ra 0.8µm or better at the machined stage. Scratches, tool marks, and handling damage are inspected under raking light before the part goes to anodize. Anything that will ghost through the anodize layer is rejected at this stage, not discovered by the customer when the batch arrives.

DFM review is included with every quote. For injection molding, DFM identifies draft angle, wall thickness, rib geometry, and gate location before T1 tooling is cut. For vacuum casting, DFM reviews parting line placement and demould access. For machined aluminium enclosures, DFM flags corner radii against tool diameter and wall thickness against fixturing pressure. Rework costs more than a DFM conversation.

Material certifications confirming polymer grade or metal alloy and temper are available on request — specify at quoting stage. Inspection reports for dimensional features are available on request. TOP PROTO operates its own facility in Dongguan, China — CNC, 3D printing, vacuum casting, injection molding, sheet metal, and surface finishing run under one roof and one ISO 9001:2015 quality system.

## Section 11: Frequently Asked Questions

**Heading:** Consumer Products Manufacturing — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What certifications does TOP PROTO hold for consumer product manufacturing?**
TOP PROTO is ISO 9001:2015 certified — the internationally recognised quality management standard requiring documented process control, material traceability, and corrective action procedures. Consumer product retail compliance marks (CE, FCC, UKCA) are product-level certifications that are the responsibility of the product manufacturer, not the component supplier. Material certifications and inspection reports for machined, cast, and molded components are available on request — specify at quoting stage.

**Q2: What tolerances can you hold on consumer product housings?**
CNC machined aluminium enclosures hold to ±0.005mm on critical features and ±0.02–0.05mm on general exterior dimensions. Vacuum cast polyurethane housings hold to ±0.2–0.8mm (ISO-2768-CL) — the correct tolerance expectation for a silicone mould process at 10–50 unit volumes. Injection molded parts hold to ±0.1mm. SLA 3D printed form study models hold to ±0.1–0.2mm. Each process has a different tolerance window — the right process for your geometry and required tolerance is confirmed in the DFM review included with every quote.

**Q3: What materials do you use for consumer product enclosures and housings?**
Aluminium 6061 is the standard for machined premium enclosures — it anodizes consistently and machines to appearance-grade Ra 0.8µm. ABS and ABS+PC blends cover the most common injection molded housings. Polycarbonate is used for transparent panels, lenses, and display bezels. TPU — thermoplastic polyurethane, a flexible engineering polymer — is overmoulded for grips and protective bumpers at Shore hardness A 40–90 depending on the application. Stainless steel 304 covers hardware elements, hinges, and clasps in premium consumer goods.

**Q4: Do you provide material certifications and inspection reports for consumer product parts?**
Material certifications and inspection reports are available on request — specify the requirement at quoting stage so documentation is included in the production plan. Dimensional inspection uses calibrated gauging and CMM for critical features on machined parts. Appearance-grade surface finish verification — surface roughness measurement and visual inspection — is available on request for anodized and painted components. Nothing ships without passing the inspection specification on the order.

**Q5: What consumer product parts do you manufacture most frequently?**
Machined aluminium enclosures for consumer electronics and wearable devices are the most common component — typically anodized to a specified colour and finish. Vacuum cast polyurethane housings for pre-production evaluation sets of 10–50 units follow. Injection molded ABS and PC housings for home appliances, audio equipment, and electronic accessories cover production-scale volumes. Sheet metal internal chassis and structural brackets support the machined and molded exterior components. SLA form study models for geometry validation before machining or tooling commitment round out the typical request mix.

**Q6: How long does it take to produce consumer product prototypes?**
SLA 3D printed form study prototypes are ready in 2–6 days. CNC machined aluminium enclosures ship in 5 days on standard lead time, or 1–3 days on rush before surface finishing. Vacuum cast sets of 10–50 units require a silicone mould from the master pattern — plan 10–14 days for the first cast set. Injection mold T1 samples arrive in 2 weeks. Upload your STEP file for a confirmed lead time alongside the DFM-reviewed quote within 24 hours.

**Q7: How much does consumer product prototype and pre-production manufacturing cost?**
SLA 3D printing is the lowest cost entry point for geometry validation — single prints accepted. CNC machined aluminium prototypes cost more but produce material properties and surface finish that translate directly to production. Vacuum casting at 10–50 units typically costs less per unit than repeated machined prototypes and less than injection tooling for that volume range. Injection molding becomes cost-competitive when volume justifies the tooling investment. Upload your STEP file and 2D drawing for a specific price on your geometry within 24 hours.

**Q8: How does TOP PROTO compare to a dedicated prototyping house for consumer product development?**
A dedicated prototyping house typically handles one or two processes — often CNC only, or 3D printing only — and hands you off to a separate supplier for vacuum casting, injection molding, or surface finishing. TOP PROTO runs CNC machining, 3D printing, vacuum casting, injection molding, sheet metal, and surface finishing in-house, all under ISO 9001:2015. That means DFM continuity across the development path — the engineer reviewing your injection molding DFM also reviewed your vacuum cast DFM — and no tolerance or specification gaps between supplier handoffs.
