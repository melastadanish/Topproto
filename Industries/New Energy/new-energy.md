## Section 3: New Energy Manufacturing at TOP PROTO

**Heading:** Precision Manufacturing for New Energy Applications

Battery systems, EV drivetrains, and fuel cell stacks place three overlapping demands on their machined and fabricated components: thermal management, high-voltage electrical isolation, and lightweight structural integrity. TOP PROTO manufactures new energy hardware in aluminium, stainless steel 316L, titanium Grade 5, copper, and PEEK for EV OEMs, fuel cell developers, and energy storage R&D teams — to tolerances of ±0.005 mm on CNC milled features — at the ISO 9001:2015 certified facility in Dongguan, China. DFM review is included with every quote.

Battery module housings must seal reliably, withstand vibration, and conduct heat away from cells. Sealing groove geometry — the channel that accepts an O-ring or gasket — must be machined to the correct depth and width or the seal compresses unevenly and leak paths form. TOP PROTO machines these features to ±0.01 mm standard, with ±0.005 mm available on critical sealing surfaces.

High-voltage isolation between conductive components uses PEEK (polyether ether ketone) — an engineering thermoplastic rated to 250°C continuous — and PTFE insulators. Both are machined in-house. An insulator machined 0.1 mm undersized on its bore can seat eccentrically against a busbar, reducing the effective creepage distance below your design spec. CNC turning and milling hold these dimensions to ±0.005 mm, verified by CMM — coordinate measuring machine — measurement before shipment.

Fuel cell bipolar plates carry flow channels — the grooves that distribute hydrogen or air across the membrane electrode assembly — typically at 0.5–2.0 mm width and 0.3–1.0 mm depth. Wire EDM cuts these channels to ±0.002–0.005 mm without the cutting forces that deform thin walls between adjacent channels. The result is a consistent flow field that performs to the modelled pressure drop, not an approximation of it.

## Section 8: Quality & Process

**Heading:** How Quality is Controlled on New Energy Parts

Sealing groove geometry on battery housings is checked by CMM — coordinate measuring machine — against your 2D drawing. Depth, width, and radius are each measured and reported. A groove 0.05 mm too shallow changes the O-ring compression ratio and shifts leak risk; the CMM report confirms the actual value before the part leaves Dongguan.

PEEK and PTFE insulator dimensions — bore diameters and seating surfaces — are verified by CMM to ±0.005 mm. Creepage and clearance distances depend on these dimensions. Any deviation from drawing is flagged and resolved before shipment, not discovered during electrical test on-site.

Wire EDM flow channel geometry on fuel cell plates is verified by contact profilometry and optical measurement of channel width and depth at multiple positions across the plate. Channel uniformity — consistency of flow field geometry across a full plate — is confirmed against your specification.

Dimensional inspection reports are available on request — specify at quoting stage. Material certifications are available on request — specify at quoting stage.
