## Section 3: Aerospace Manufacturing at TOP PROTO

**Heading:** Precision Manufacturing for Aerospace

TOP PROTO machines aerospace prototype and low-volume parts to tolerances of ±0.005mm at its Dongguan, China facility — covering aluminium 7075, titanium Grade 5, stainless 316L, and Inconel for structural, avionics, and hydraulic applications. ISO 9001:2015 certification governs every order: documented process control, material traceability from stock to finished part, and dimensional verification available on request. Every quote includes a DFM review — geometry, material selection, and tolerance feasibility checked before a single chip is cut.

Aerospace parts carry two requirements that general machining cannot meet: geometric precision across complex multi-surface features, and a documented evidence chain that proves each dimension was held. 5-axis simultaneous machining — where the cutting tool moves across five axes in a single clamping — eliminates the repositioning transfers that accumulate error in 3-axis work. Each repositioning introduces a datum shift, and datum shifts stack tolerances in the wrong direction on multi-surface structural parts.

Wire EDM (electrical discharge machining — material removed by controlled electrical discharge between a wire electrode and the workpiece, with no cutting force applied) reaches ±0.002–0.005mm on profiles, slots, and apertures that a milling cutter cannot access. Surface finish after EDM reaches Ra 0.4µm — sufficient for fuel system fittings, hydraulic ports, and precision apertures without secondary grinding. Inconel and titanium, which resist standard end mills, are well-suited to EDM because the process is independent of material hardness.

For avionics housings and sensor enclosures, SLA (stereolithography — photopolymer resin cured layer by layer under UV light) produces form-and-fit prototypes to ±0.1–0.2mm with fine surface detail — fast enough to validate a housing design before committing to a machined aluminium part. Prototype runs start at a single part. No minimum order quantity applies.

## Section 7: Certifications Relevant to Aerospace

**Heading:** Certifications and Quality Standards for Aerospace

**AS9100D** is the aerospace quality management standard used by prime contractors and Tier 1 suppliers. It extends ISO 9001:2015 with requirements for first-article inspection (FAIR — a documented dimensional and material verification of the first part produced), full traceability of materials and processes through the supply chain, configuration management, and risk-based process control. Many aerospace programmes require AS9100D supplier registration as a condition of purchase order award.

**NADCAP** (National Aerospace and Defense Contractors Accreditation Program) covers special processes — heat treatment, non-destructive testing, chemical processing — that aerospace parts frequently require after machining.

TOP PROTO holds **ISO 9001:2015** certification, which covers documented quality management, process control, traceability, and corrective action. AS9100D and NADCAP are not currently held as registered certifications. For projects that specify AS9100D compliance — or that require FAIR documentation, material traceability reports, or NADCAP-accredited post-processing — raise these requirements at the quoting stage. The engineering team will confirm what is directly supported and identify any additional steps needed.

CMM (coordinate measuring machine — measures part geometry to micron-level accuracy against your drawing dimensions) inspection is available on request. Specify CMM verification and first-article documentation when submitting your quote.
