## Section 3: Automotive Manufacturing at TOP PROTO

**Heading:** Precision Manufacturing for Automotive

TOP PROTO produces automotive prototype and low-volume parts at its Dongguan, China facility — CNC machining, injection molding, vacuum casting, sheet metal, and 3D printing under one ISO 9001:2015 certified quality system. Tolerances to ±0.005mm on machined metal components, ±0.1mm on injection molded plastic parts. Every quote includes a DFM review — geometry, material, draft angles, and tolerance stack all checked before tooling is cut or metal is machined.

Automotive component development moves in compressed cycles. A powertrain bracket design needs to be validated in aluminium before the programme commits to tooling. A new interior trim panel needs to be evaluated in the final material — not a printed stand-in — before the colour and material sign-off. Vacuum casting (polyurethane parts cast from a silicone mould taken from a machined master pattern) produces low-volume plastic parts in production-representative materials, with surface finish and Shore hardness that a 3D print cannot replicate. For evaluation runs of 5–50 parts, vacuum casting bridges the gap between prototype and injection-molded production without the T1 tooling investment.

For parts that will go to injection molding production, prototype tooling in aluminium reaches T1 samples in 2 weeks — giving engineering teams a production-representative part for functional validation and assembly fit checks before the hardened production tool is committed. IATF 16949 — the automotive quality management standard — defines processes like PPAP (Production Part Approval Process, a documented approval of parts produced from the production process and tooling), APQP (Advanced Product Quality Planning), and MSA (Measurement System Analysis) that govern supplier qualification in volume automotive programmes. These processes can be discussed at the quoting stage for projects that require them.

Sheet metal prototype parts for body and structural applications ship in 3 days. Aluminium 6061 and steel 4140 machined brackets are available in 5 days standard. Prototype plastic components by SLA (stereolithography — photopolymer resin cured layer by layer under UV light) are ready in 2–6 days for design validation before tooling is ordered.

## Section 7: Certifications Relevant to Automotive

**Heading:** Certifications and Quality Standards for Automotive

**IATF 16949** is the automotive-specific quality management standard. It extends ISO 9001:2015 with requirements for PPAP (Production Part Approval Process — a documented approval confirming that the production process can consistently produce parts within specification), APQP (Advanced Product Quality Planning — a structured approach to defining and establishing the steps needed to ensure a product meets customer requirements), and MSA (Measurement System Analysis — a study of measurement variation to confirm that inspection equipment gives reliable results). Tier 1 suppliers and OEMs typically require IATF 16949 registration from production part suppliers.

TOP PROTO holds **ISO 9001:2015** certification, which covers documented quality management, process control, and corrective action. IATF 16949 is not currently held as a registered certification. For automotive prototype and low-volume projects that require specific PPAP documentation, inspection plans, or APQP deliverables, raise these requirements at the quoting stage — the engineering team will confirm what is supported and what additional documentation can be provided.

DFM (design for manufacturability) review is included with every quote — draft angles, wall thickness, gate location, and tolerance stack are checked before tooling is ordered for injection molded parts. For machined parts, fixturing strategy, datum selection, and critical feature accessibility are reviewed at the same time.

## Section 8: Quality & Process

**Heading:** Quality Steps That Matter for Automotive Parts

Every order follows a documented process from material receipt through to shipment. Material traceability — confirming alloy grade or resin specification — is maintained across all orders and certification documentation is available on request at the quoting stage. For injection molded parts, cavity sampling and dimensional check are performed before shipment.

DFM review is included with every quote — not charged separately and not optional. For injection molded components, the review checks draft angles (the slight taper required on vertical walls so a part releases cleanly from the mould tool), gate location, wall thickness uniformity, and potential sink marks (depressions caused by uneven cooling where wall thickness changes). Catching these before T1 tooling saves rework cost that cannot be recovered from a hardened tool.

Dimensional inspection reports and material certifications are available on request — specify at the quoting stage. In-process inspection is performed at critical operations. For parts with GD&T (geometric dimensioning and tolerancing — a drawing language that defines allowable variation in form, position, and size) requirements, confirm these on your drawing at submission.

## Section 11: FAQ

**Heading:** Automotive Manufacturing — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What manufacturing certifications does TOP PROTO hold for automotive?**

TOP PROTO holds ISO 9001:2015 certification, covering documented quality management, process control, and corrective action. IATF 16949 is the automotive-specific quality standard — it adds requirements for PPAP, APQP, and MSA that Tier 1 and OEM suppliers are typically required to hold. TOP PROTO does not currently hold IATF 16949 as a registered certification. For automotive projects that require specific PPAP documentation, control plans, or APQP deliverables, discuss these requirements at the quoting stage — the team will confirm what is directly supported.

**Q2: What tolerances can you achieve for automotive parts?**

CNC machined metal parts hold ±0.01mm standard and ±0.005mm on critical features — bore positions, mating surfaces, and sealing diameters. Injection molded parts hold ±0.1mm. Vacuum cast polyurethane parts are produced to ±0.2–0.8mm (ISO 2768-CL). Sheet metal parts are cut and bent to ±0.2mm (ISO 2768-c) with ±1.0° bend angle. For any feature with a tighter requirement, include the dimension and tolerance on your drawing — the DFM review will confirm feasibility.

**Q3: What materials do you machine for automotive?**

Aluminium 6061-T6 for lightweight structural brackets and housings, steel 4140 for drivetrain and suspension components carrying cyclic loads, stainless 304 for corrosion-exposed parts, and ABS/PC for interior and sensor housing applications. Injection molding also supports PP, nylon, and POM depending on the functional requirement. Confirm your specific material grade at quoting — the engineering team will flag any machinability or tooling considerations before the order starts.

**Q4: Do you provide material certifications and inspection reports for automotive orders?**

Dimensional inspection reports and material certifications are available on request — specify these requirements at the quoting stage. For injection molded parts, cavity dimensional data is available on request. These are not included by default in every shipment; call them out when submitting your quote. For projects with a formal inspection plan or control plan requirement, discuss this at quoting stage.

**Q5: What automotive parts does TOP PROTO manufacture most frequently?**

Powertrain mounting brackets, suspension component prototypes, intake manifold bodies, interior trim panel evaluation sets, sensor housings, and gearbox enclosures are the most common part types. CNC machining covers structural metal parts; vacuum casting covers plastic validation sets; injection molding covers production-representative T1 samples; sheet metal covers brackets and enclosure panels.

**Q6: What is the lead time for automotive prototype parts?**

CNC prototype parts in aluminium and steel ship in 5 days standard, 1–3 days on rush. Sheet metal prototypes ship in 3 days. Vacuum cast polyurethane parts are ready in 5–10 days including mould fabrication and casting. Injection molded T1 samples from aluminium prototype tooling are available in 2 weeks. SLA 3D printing prototypes are ready in 2–6 days. Upload your STEP file and specify your required delivery date — the quote will confirm lead time for your geometry, material, and process.

**Q7: What is the minimum order quantity for automotive parts?**

No minimum order quantity applies to prototype and low-volume orders. A single machined part, a single sheet metal bracket, or a short vacuum cast run of 5–10 parts — all accepted without a batch commitment. Injection molding T1 samples are quoted per-tool regardless of sample quantity.

**Q8: How does vacuum casting compare to injection molding for automotive plastic prototype parts?**

Vacuum casting uses a silicone mould (made from a machined master pattern) to cast polyurethane parts — mould cost is low, lead time is 5–10 days, and materials match the Shore hardness and visual appearance of production ABS or PP. Injection molding uses a hard aluminium or steel tool — higher tooling cost, 2-week T1 lead time, but produces parts from the actual production resin in the production process. For 5–50 validation parts before tooling is committed, vacuum casting is faster and lower cost. For T1 functional sign-off and assembly approval in the production material, injection molding is the right step.
