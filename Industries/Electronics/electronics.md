## Section 3: Electronics Manufacturing at TOP PROTO

**Heading:** Precision Manufacturing for Electronics Hardware

Electronics enclosures, heatsinks, and PCB fixtures share three demands that separate acceptable parts from ones that actually work: dimensional accuracy at mating interfaces, thermal performance dictated by fin geometry, and EMI shielding effectiveness tied to conductivity and surface finish. TOP PROTO manufactures electronics hardware in aluminium 6061, copper C101, stainless steel 304, and engineering plastics — to tolerances of ±0.005 mm on CNC milled features — for hardware engineers and R&D teams at the Dongguan, China facility. ISO 9001:2015 governs every order.

Heatsink performance depends on fin pitch, fin height, and base thickness — all held to your drawing. A fin array machined 0.2 mm wider than specified reduces airflow resistance more than your thermal model predicted. TOP PROTO machines heatsink profiles in aluminium 6061 with as-machined surface finish at Ra 3.2 µm, improving to Ra 0.8 µm where polished surfaces are specified for anodised contact faces.

EMI shielding — suppressing electromagnetic interference by surrounding sensitive circuits with a conductive enclosure — requires both material selection and tight tolerances at seam interfaces. Copper C101 provides the highest conductivity for RF shielding applications. Stainless steel 304 provides structural rigidity for chassis-level shielding. Wire EDM cuts precision apertures and slots in shield panels to ±0.002–0.005 mm without distorting the surrounding sheet.

PCB test fixtures carry some of the tightest positional tolerances in electronics manufacture. Spring-loaded pin positions must align to PCB pad centres — typically at 1.27 mm or 2.54 mm pitch — and the fixture plate must sit flat against the board surface. CNC machining holds these positions to ±0.01 mm standard, with ±0.005 mm available for fine-pitch applications.

## Section 8: Quality & Process

**Heading:** How Quality is Controlled on Electronics Parts

Connector interfaces on electronics enclosures are checked against your 2D drawing using CMM — coordinate measuring machine — equipment that verifies position and geometry to micron accuracy. Heatsink fin pitch and base flatness are confirmed dimensionally before anodising. PCB fixture hole positions are checked at 100% for fine-pitch applications.

Surface finish (Ra) verification matters for electronics parts more than most hardware categories. An anodised heatsink base at Ra 3.2 µm transfers less heat to a thermal pad than one finished to Ra 0.8 µm. Both values are confirmed by profilometer on request. Conductive surface treatments — where the aluminium remains bare for EMI grounding — are called out on the drawing and verified against the finish spec before shipment.

Bend geometry on sheet metal EMI shields is verified for angle and flange length. Sheet metal parts meet ±0.2 mm (ISO 2768-c) positional tolerance and ±1.0° bend tolerance as standard. Tighter requirements are quoted individually with fixturing details.

Dimensional inspection reports are available on request — specify at quoting stage. Material certifications are available on request — specify at quoting stage.
