## Section 3: What Is Sheet Metal Fabrication

**Design:** 2-column — text left, process illustration right. White background.
**Heading:** Sheet Metal Fabrication for Prototypes and Low-Volume Parts

Sheet metal fabrication converts flat metal stock into three-dimensional parts using cutting and forming operations. At TOP PROTO we run two core processes — laser cutting and CNC bending — to produce brackets, enclosures, panels, frames, and structural components from prototype quantities upward.

Laser cutting uses a focused high-power laser to cut flat blanks from sheet stock with precision. Kerf width is typically 0.1–0.3mm. Cut quality on aluminium and stainless steel is clean enough to require no secondary deburring on most profiles. Complex profiles, slots, and hole patterns cut in a single setup.

CNC bending uses a press brake with precision tooling to form flat blanks into three-dimensional shapes. Bend angle and bend radius are controlled to tight tolerances. We program bends in sequence to achieve finished geometry — no fixturing, no secondary operations.

The combination of laser cutting and CNC bending handles the majority of enclosure, bracket, and panel work that engineers prototype. Both processes share the same flat DXF source — one file covers both cut profile and bend programme.

## Section 8: Design Guidelines

**Design:** Table format. White background.
**Heading:** Sheet Metal Design Guidelines

| Feature | Recommendation | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Min hole diameter | 1.2× sheet thickness | Smaller holes punch-tear or require secondary drilling |
| Hole to edge distance | 2× sheet thickness minimum | Prevents material tearing near cut edge |
| Min flange length | 3× sheet thickness | Shorter flanges slip under press brake tooling |
| Min bend radius | 1× material thickness | Tighter radii cause cracking on some alloys |
| Relief cuts at bend corners | Required at >90° bends | Without relief, material tears at inside corner |
| Consistent bend direction | Preferred — reduces setups | Multiple bend directions add handling and cost |
| Thread holes | Tap after bending, not before | Tapping a flat blank risks thread distortion during bending |
| Tolerances on mating flanges | State explicitly in drawing | Default sheet metal tolerance is ±0.2mm — tighter requires note |
| File format | DXF with fully unfolded blank | Folded 3D STEP also accepted — we unfold if DXF not provided |

## Section 10: Quality & Certifications (RS-01)
**Design:** RS-01 block. Dark navy background. White text.

**Pre-Production:**
Every sheet metal order includes a DFM review. We check your DXF against fabrication minimums — hole sizes, edge distances, bend radii, flange lengths. We flag issues before cutting starts, not after. Material is verified against the specified grade and thickness.

**In-Production:**
First-off parts are measured against the flat blank drawing before proceeding to bending. Bend angles are verified on the first part with a digital protractor. Critical hole positions are spot-checked with CMM or digital height gauge.

**Final:**
Completed parts are inspected against the STEP reference. Dimensional report available on request [VERIFY]. Material certification available on request [VERIFY]. Parts are packed individually — sheet metal edges wrapped to prevent handling damage.
