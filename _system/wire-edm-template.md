## Section 3b: Wire EDM vs CNC Machining
**Design:** 2-column comparison. White background.
**Heading:** Wire EDM vs CNC Machining

**Use Wire EDM When:**
- Material is hardened and can't be milled without tool wear (D2, H13, tungsten carbide)
- Internal corner radius must be smaller than a milling tool can achieve (< 0.1mm)
- Thin ribs or delicate features would deflect under milling cutting forces
- Taper angles are required in the cut geometry
- Part requires zero burr on cut edges

**Use CNC Machining When:**
- Material is in soft/annealed state (machine first, then harden)
- 3D surface geometry is required (wire EDM cuts 2D profiles and tapers only)
- Part needs features that aren't on a single cut axis
- Material is non-conductive
